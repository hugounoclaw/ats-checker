Warehouse Supervisor ATS Resume Checklist (2026)

12 ATS-resume checks Warehouse Supervisors need to pass in 2026, the keywords recruiters scan for, and three role-specific resume bullets to copy.

Warehouse Supervisor resumes are filtered hard for WMS proficiency, OSHA literacy, headcount-per-shift, and safety metrics. Manhattan Associates, HighJump (now Korber), SAP EWM, Blue Yonder (JDA), and Oracle WMS Cloud are the platform names that ATS scans look for. If the resume only says 'WMS', the recruiter assumes you ran a clipboard and a smile, not a real system.

Hiring managers expect specific operational metrics: picks-per-hour, dock-to-stock cycle time, OTIF or shipping accuracy, units per labor hour (UPH), and incident rate (TRIR or DART). Forklift certification, OSHA 10 or 30, hazmat handling (49 CFR), and any inventory-cycle-count program ownership signal a real floor leader. Headcount per shift, shift cadence (1st/2nd/3rd), and facility size in square feet anchor your scope.

The 12-point ATS checklist for Warehouse Supervisors

  1. Name the WMS by vendorManhattan Active WM, Korber/HighJump, SAP EWM, Blue Yonder WMS, Oracle WMS Cloud. Specific WMS naming is the #1 screening keyword for warehouse hiring.
  2. State facility size in square feet300K-sq-ft, 1.2M-sq-ft, etc. Facility scale maps to operational complexity and pay tier; recruiters use square footage as a scope shortcut.
  3. Quantify headcount per shift and total spanDirect reports per shift plus total span (e.g., '34 associates per shift across 3 shifts, 102 total'). Span is core to supervisor vs lead vs manager tiering.
  4. List OSHA 10 or OSHA 30 certificationOSHA 30 is the senior-supervisor expectation; OSHA 10 is the floor. Hazmat (49 CFR) and any DOT certs strengthen multi-modal facility profiles.
  5. Show forklift and PIT certificationsSit-down, reach truck, order picker, cherry picker — and whether you're a certified PIT trainer. Trainer status signals leadership beyond solo operation.
  6. Include picks-per-hour or UPH metricFloor-level productivity metrics with before/after improvement. 'Lifted UPH from 78 to 112 over 8 months via slotting redesign' is highly screenable.
  7. Detail safety incident rate (TRIR/DART)Recordable incident rate and any consecutive injury-free-day streaks. Safety record is a top-three screen at all Tier-1 3PLs and retail DCs.
  8. Show dock-to-stock cycle and OTIFReceiving-to-putaway hours and outbound on-time-in-full percentage. End-to-end cycle metrics prove you understand flow beyond one zone.
  9. Quantify inventory accuracy and cycle countsAnnual cycle-count program coverage, inventory accuracy percentage, and shrink/loss reduction. Inventory ownership separates supervisor from lead roles.
  10. Reference shift management cadence1st, 2nd, 3rd, or weekend shift — and any shift-bridge or hand-off processes. Multi-shift coordination experience commands premium pay.
  11. List labor-management or workforce-planning systemsKronos, UKG Pro WFM, Manhattan LMS, or JDA Workforce. LMS fluency shows you can run engineered labor standards, not just gut-feel scheduling.
  12. Avoid generic 'oversaw warehouse operations' linesWarehouse hiring is metric-driven. Each bullet needs UPH, accuracy, safety, cost, or headcount math attached.

Common ATS rejection reasons for Warehouse Supervisors

✗ WMS not named specifically
Fix:Spell out Manhattan, Korber, SAP EWM, or Blue Yonder. Generic 'WMS' fails recruiter Boolean filters which key on vendor names.
✗ No facility size or shift headcount
Fix:Add square footage and associates per shift. Without scope numbers, supervisors read as leads and get filtered into lower bands.
✗ Safety record missing
Fix:Add TRIR, DART, or injury-free-day streaks. Safety is a knockout criterion at most large DCs and 3PLs.
✗ Productivity claims without UPH or PPH
Fix:Convert 'improved productivity' to 'lifted UPH from X to Y'. Engineered-labor metrics are what hiring managers actually use to evaluate.
✗ Forklift listed without specific class
Fix:Specify equipment classes (sit-down, reach, order picker) and trainer status. Trainer-certified supervisors carry a meaningful premium.

FAQ — Warehouse Supervisor ATS questions

Do I need WMS experience to apply for supervisor roles?

Most enterprise DCs and 3PLs require it — even smaller fulfillment centers run on Manhattan, Korber, or NetSuite WMS now. If your prior facility was RF-and-paper, frame any system migration experience prominently and name any WMS you've used as an end user.

How do I show safety leadership if I'm not yet OSHA 30 certified?

List OSHA 10, any in-house safety-committee role, near-miss reporting programs you ran, and committed timeline to OSHA 30 if pursuing it. Safety leadership is demonstrated through behaviors as much as certifications.

Are inbound/outbound metrics like OTIF actually checked on resumes?

Yes. OTIF is a top-three operational KPI at retail DCs (especially Walmart and Amazon vendor facilities) and is one of the first filters recruiters apply when calibrating supervisor candidates.

Should I include union experience if I supervised a union floor?

Absolutely. Union supervision is a distinct skill — grievance handling, steward relationships, CBA literacy. Many hiring managers actively prefer or require it for unionized facilities; some non-union shops view it neutrally.

How do I quantify productivity if my facility had no engineered standards?

Use comparative deltas — 'doubled outbound throughput from 2,200 to 4,400 units per shift over 6 months' or 'cut overtime hours 38%'. Absolute UPH is best, but relative improvements still demonstrate competence.
