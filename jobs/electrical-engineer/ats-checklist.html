Electrical Engineer ATS Resume Checklist (2026)

12 ATS-resume checks Electrical Engineers need to pass in 2026, the keywords recruiters scan for, and three role-specific resume bullets to copy.

Electrical engineering resumes get filtered by ATS keyword stacks and then scrutinized by hiring managers looking for tool fluency, standards literacy, and product-launch evidence. Recruiters at hardware companies want to see specific EDA tools (Altium, KiCad, Cadence), simulation environments (MATLAB/Simulink, LTspice), and the IEEE/UL/IPC standards your designs were tested against.

Mid-level and senior candidates should signal PE licensure or EIT progress, FPGA/embedded experience with Verilog or VHDL, and concrete board bring-up or power-electronics outcomes. Generic 'designed circuits' bullets get cut. Reviewers want layer counts, switching frequencies, efficiency percentages, and the certification body that signed off on the product.

The 12-point ATS checklist for Electrical Engineers

  1. List PE license or EIT status with stateSpell out 'Professional Engineer (PE), Electrical, California #E12345' or 'Engineer-in-Training (EIT), passed FE Electrical 2024'. Recruiters search by state postnominal and exam status; abbreviations alone get missed.
  2. Name EDA tools with version and use caseWrite 'Altium Designer 23 (12-layer HDI), KiCad 7, Cadence Allegro' instead of 'PCB software'. Hiring managers filter for the exact tool their team uses and the complexity you handled.
  3. Specify PCB layer count, stackup, and pourQuantify boards as '8-layer impedance-controlled, 100-ohm differential pairs, 0.5mm BGA fanout' so reviewers know you handled DDR routing, controlled impedance, and high-density assembly, not just two-layer breakout boards.
  4. Call out FPGA HDL with target device familyList 'Verilog/VHDL on Xilinx Zynq-7020, Intel Cyclone V, Lattice iCE40' with synthesis tools (Vivado, Quartus). FPGA roles screen on device family experience, not just the language.
  5. Quote IEEE, UL, CE, FCC, and IPC standardsName 'IPC-A-610 Class 3, IPC-2221, UL 60950, FCC Part 15 Subpart B, IEC 61010, IEEE 1588 PTP'. ATS keyword lists for regulated hardware roles are built from these exact identifiers.
  6. Show power-electronics specs with unitsQuantify 'designed 48V-to-12V synchronous buck, 96% peak efficiency at 30A, 500kHz switching' rather than 'worked on power supplies'. Power roles want topology, efficiency, switching frequency, and load.
  7. List MATLAB and Simulink with toolboxes usedSpecify 'Simulink with Simscape Electrical, Control System Toolbox, HDL Coder' so signal-processing and motor-control teams see relevant model-based design history, not just generic MATLAB exposure.
  8. Document signal-integrity and EMC complianceMention 'pre-compliance EMC testing, ANSYS SIwave for SI analysis, passed FCC Class B at first attempt'. SI/EMC is where junior boards fail certification; proof of first-pass compliance is rare and valuable.
  9. Include test equipment and bring-up toolsList 'Keysight DSOX oscilloscope, Rohde & Schwarz spectrum analyzer, BERT testers, JTAG/SWD debuggers'. Bring-up engineers are filtered on lab fluency, not just CAD time.
  10. Quantify production volume and yieldWrite '12kU annual production, 99.2% first-pass yield, $14 BOM cost' to prove the design survived manufacturing transfer. Many resumes stop at prototype; hiring managers want NPI evidence.
  11. Use ECAD-to-MCAD collaboration keywordsMention 'STEP file handoff, IDF/IDX co-design with SolidWorks, ECAD-MCAD reviews per IPC-7351'. Hardware teams need engineers who interface with mechanical, not solo board-house designers.
  12. Detail firmware or driver work if applicableNote 'wrote STM32 HAL drivers in C, Linux device tree edits for custom IMX8 carrier'. Embedded-leaning EE roles screen for at least one production firmware contribution alongside the schematic.

Common ATS rejection reasons for Electrical Engineers

✗ Lists 'PCB design' but no EDA tool or layer count
Fix:Replace with 'Altium Designer 23, 10-layer controlled-impedance board, 0.4mm BGA' so ATS and reviewers see the tool and complexity.
✗ PE license abbreviated or buried under 'Skills'
Fix:Add a dedicated 'Licensure' line: 'Professional Engineer (PE), Electrical, Texas #98765, active 2022-present' so recruiters can grep by state.
✗ FPGA work claimed without HDL or device family
Fix:Specify 'Verilog on Xilinx Artix-7, AXI4 bus mastering, simulation in Vivado XSim' so FPGA managers see real RTL experience.
✗ No certification bodies named on shipped products
Fix:List 'product passed UL 62368-1, FCC Part 15 Class B, CE EMC Directive 2014/30/EU' to prove design survived a real cert lab.
✗ Generic 'tested circuits' with no instruments named
Fix:Add 'characterized loop response on Keysight E5061B VNA, validated EMI on Rohde & Schwarz ESR3' so lab fluency is concrete.
✗ Power supply work missing efficiency, topology, switching frequency
Fix:Quantify 'GaN-based 400V LLC, 350W, 97% efficiency at 50% load, 500kHz' so power-electronics screeners can rank the design difficulty.

FAQ — Electrical Engineer ATS questions

Should I list my PE license if it is in a different state than the job?

Yes. List it with the state and number, then add 'comity/reciprocity eligible' if the target state accepts NCEES records. Recruiters search for 'PE' broadly; the state confirms it is real and reciprocity language addresses the obvious next question.

Do I need to list every EDA tool I have ever used?

No. List the two or three you can sit down and use today at a senior level, with version numbers. Add a 'familiar with' line for the rest. Padding the tool list dilutes keyword density and triggers technical-screen failures when interviewers test depth.

How do I show FPGA work if my code is under NDA?

Describe the functional block, interface, clock rate, and device family without naming the customer: '10GbE MAC, AXI4-Stream, 156MHz on Xilinx Kintex-7, 38% LUT utilization.' Numbers and protocols are not confidential and prove real RTL ownership.

Is a power-electronics role expecting magnetics design experience?

Yes for senior roles. Mention transformer or inductor design in tools like Ansys PExprt, Magnetics Designer, or hand calculations per McLyman. List core material (ferrite N87, powdered iron), Bsat headroom, and copper loss to prove you understand the magnetics, not just the IC.

Should I include MATLAB if I only used it in school?

Only if you can defend a specific toolbox today. List 'MATLAB (Control System Toolbox, Simulink)' if you can model a closed-loop system on a whiteboard. Otherwise drop it; interviewers will ask, and a missed MATLAB question on a resume claim is a fast rejection.
