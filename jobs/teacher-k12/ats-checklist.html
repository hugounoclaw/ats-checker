K-12 Teacher ATS Resume Checklist (2026)

12 ATS-resume checks K-12 Teachers need to pass in 2026, the keywords recruiters scan for, and three role-specific resume bullets to copy.

K-12 teacher resumes are screened by district HR systems (Frontline, Applitrack, TalentEd) plus principal-level review. The first filter is state credential — type (Single Subject, Multiple Subject, Special Education) and authorizations (CLAD, BCLAD, EL, SPED). The second is grade-level and subject specifics: 'taught middle school' is far weaker than '7th grade math, integrated I, 32-student classes, Title I site'.

Districts increasingly want measurable student-outcome evidence — SBAC/state-test growth percentage, NWEA MAP gains, IB or AP pass rates, graduation or promotion rates. CCSS or NGSS alignment, classroom-management methodology (PBIS, Responsive Classroom, Restorative Practices), IEP/504 caseload experience, and EL/MLL student percentages tell principals you can teach the kids actually in the room, not a hypothetical class.

The 12-point ATS checklist for K-12 Teachers

  1. List state teaching credential type and statusSingle Subject (Math), Multiple Subject, Education Specialist (Mild/Moderate) — plus state, credential type (Preliminary vs Clear), and expiration. Credential is the knockout filter.
  2. Specify grade level and subject taught'7th grade integrated math' and '11th grade AP US History' are far more screenable than 'middle school' or 'high school social studies'. Principal hires by exact slot.
  3. State school type and demographicsTitle I, charter, magnet, private, IB, or comprehensive — plus FRPL percentage and ELL percentage if relevant. Equity-focused districts screen for demographic match.
  4. Quantify class size and total student loadClass size and number of sections (e.g., '5 sections of 32 students, 160 total caseload'). Workload context separates self-contained from secondary teachers.
  5. Show student growth on state assessmentSBAC, STAAR, NY State Tests, Florida FAST — list growth percentage or percentile improvement. NWEA MAP RIT growth works for districts without state-test public reporting.
  6. List CCSS, NGSS, or state-standard fluencyCommon Core State Standards, NGSS for science, CA Math Framework, etc. Standards literacy is assumed but specific framework naming still scores.
  7. Cover IEP/504 caseload and SPED collaborationNumber of IEPs and 504s implemented, IEP-meeting participation, co-teaching model used (push-in, pull-out, full inclusion). SPED literacy is a top-five screen now.
  8. Detail classroom-management methodologyPBIS, Responsive Classroom, Restorative Practices, CHAMPS, Love & Logic. Naming the framework signals trained practice over improvisation.
  9. Document EL/MLL student supportNumber of English Learner students, SDAIE strategies used, BCLAD or EL authorization, and reclassification rates if available.
  10. Show curriculum or PLC leadershipDepartment chair, grade-level lead, PLC facilitator, curriculum-mapping work. Teacher-leader roles signal promotability.
  11. List edtech platforms usedGoogle Classroom, Canvas, Schoology, IXL, Khan Academy, NewSela, DreamBox, Lexia. Edtech fluency reduces principal training overhead.
  12. Avoid 'passionate educator' fillerEvery district resume claims passion. Replace with student-outcome numbers, methodology specifics, and credential precision.

Common ATS rejection reasons for K-12 Teachers

✗ Credential type or state not specified
Fix:Put credential type, authorizations, and state in the resume header. Districts have hundreds of applicants — credential mismatch is filtered instantly.
✗ Grade level too vague ('elementary')
Fix:Specify exact grade(s) and subject(s). Principals hire for specific openings; '3rd grade self-contained, dual-immersion Spanish' is matchable, 'elementary' is not.
✗ No student-outcome data
Fix:Add SBAC growth, MAP gains, AP pass rate, or graduation rate. Outcome-blind resumes lose to outcome-quantified peers in every modern hiring rubric.
✗ IEP/504 work omitted
Fix:List caseload size and collaboration model. SPED inclusion experience is required for nearly all GenEd roles now.
✗ Classroom management described without methodology
Fix:Name PBIS, Responsive Classroom, Restorative Practices, or similar. Methodology naming signals trained practice and supports principal trust.

FAQ — K-12 Teacher ATS questions

Should I include student test scores on my resume?

Yes, in aggregate and as growth rather than absolute scores. 'Improved class SBAC math scores from 42% to 67% meeting standard over 2 years' is appropriate. Never name individual students or use scores tied to identifiable cohorts in ways that would breach FERPA.

How do I show classroom management without sounding like a behavior cop?

Name your methodology (PBIS, Responsive Classroom, Restorative Practices) and pair with outcome — referrals down, attendance up, instructional minutes preserved. Framework plus outcome reads as skilled rather than punitive.

Do I list every credential authorization?

List all currently held — Single/Multiple Subject, supplementary authorizations (introductory subject), CLAD/BCLAD, EL, SPED add-ons, and any National Board Certification. Districts often need a specific authorization for a specific opening.

Should new teachers include student-teaching placements?

Absolutely — student teaching is your only classroom evidence early on. List host school type, grade, subject, cooperating teacher's tenure, and any measurable student outcomes from your unit plans.

How do I show edtech fluency without listing every app?

List the LMS (Google Classroom, Canvas, Schoology) plus 2-3 platforms genuinely used for instruction (IXL, DreamBox, Lexia, NewSela). Quality over quantity — naming 15 tools signals shallow use of all of them.
