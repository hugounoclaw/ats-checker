UX Designer ATS Resume Checklist (2026)

12 ATS-resume checks UX Designers need to pass in 2026, the keywords recruiters scan for, and three role-specific resume bullets to copy.

UX Designer hiring is one of the few tech tracks where ATS keyword matching is genuinely secondary to portfolio quality. That said, the resume still has to clear the keyword screen first, especially at large product companies where Figma, design systems, and accessibility terms are required matches. Without a working portfolio link the resume is functionally incomplete; without case-study depth in the portfolio, the resume is irrelevant.

In 2026 the role has consolidated around Figma as the standard tool, with design systems thinking, accessibility (WCAG 2.2), and usability-testing rigor as the three pillars hiring managers screen on. The portfolio carries the interview; the resume should orient the reviewer to which case studies prove which competencies.

The 12-point ATS checklist for UX Designers

  1. Include a clickable portfolio URL in the headerNo portfolio means no callback for product UX roles. Use a clean custom domain or a Notion/Read.cv page; password-gated portfolios slow recruiters down and cost interviews.
  2. List 3-4 case-study titles inline on the resumeTreat case studies as anchor projects: Redesigned onboarding for a B2B fintech, 38 percent activation lift. The resume should tell the reviewer which study to open first.
  3. Name your design tool stack preciselyFigma (including Auto Layout, Variables, Dev Mode), FigJam, Maze, Dovetail. Tool literacy with sub-feature mention separates senior from junior designers in the first scan.
  4. Show usability-testing volume and methodRan 14 moderated usability sessions on Lookback with n=6 per round across 3 personas. Method + sample size signals research literacy, not just designer-as-tester.
  5. Reference accessibility with a standard versionShipped to WCAG 2.2 AA across 22 flows, validated with axe DevTools and screen reader passes. Accessibility is now procurement-relevant in 2026 (especially EU Accessibility Act compliance).
  6. Quote a design-system contribution countAuthored 38 components in the shared Figma library used by 14 designers and 60 engineers. Design system contribution scope distinguishes product designers from feature designers.
  7. Show one measurable product outcome per major bulletCut signup drop-off from 41 percent to 22 percent across the 4-step flow. Design impact is hardest to quantify; one number per role flips you from craft to outcomes designer.
  8. Mention discovery research, not just delivery designConducted 18 customer discovery interviews and synthesized 6 jobs-to-be-done across the SMB segment. Discovery work is undervalued on resumes and high-signal for staff roles.
  9. Reference Nielsen heuristics or another framework if relevantLed 4 heuristic evaluations applying Nielsen's 10 across 12 admin screens. Frameworks ground subjective claims and reassure design-mature hiring teams.
  10. Cross-functional collaboration with engineering and PMPaired daily with 6 engineers via Figma Dev Mode, shipped 31 features over 4 quarters. Designers who ship in lockstep with eng outpace pure spec throwers in modern PDLs.
  11. Show prototyping fidelity rangeFrom low-fi FigJam wireflows through hi-fi prototypes with Figma variables and prototype-only animations. Fidelity range tells reviewers you can match the moment, not over-polish too early.
  12. List domain or vertical depth where you have itFintech onboarding KYC flows, healthcare patient intake, B2B SaaS admin. Domain depth shortens ramp time and is increasingly valued over generalist breadth at staff level.

Common ATS rejection reasons for UX Designers

✗ No portfolio link on the resume header
Fix:Add a clickable URL on line one and ensure it loads without login on mobile in under 3 seconds.
✗ Portfolio has only screenshots, no problem statement or outcome
Fix:Rewrite top 3 case studies with problem, constraints, process, prototype, test, and measured outcome.
✗ Lists 12 tools (Figma, Sketch, XD, InVision, Axure, Balsamiq...)
Fix:Cut to Figma primary plus one secondary; tool sprawl reads as juniorism in 2026 since Figma has consolidated the market.
✗ No mention of accessibility anywhere
Fix:Add WCAG 2.2 AA references and at least one accessibility audit bullet; procurement teams increasingly require it on shipped work.
✗ Bullets describe deliverables (wireframes, mockups) but no user outcome
Fix:Reframe each bullet around the user problem solved and the metric moved, not the artifact produced.

FAQ — UX Designer ATS questions

Is a portfolio site mandatory or is a Figma community page enough?

A real portfolio site (custom domain, Read.cv, Notion site, Framer) is strongly preferred and most product companies require it. Figma community pages do not show your process and read as junior. Invest one weekend in a clean three-case-study site; it pays back fast.

Should I password-protect my portfolio for NDA work?

Have a public version with at least one or two unblocked case studies plus a password-gated section for NDA work. A fully gated portfolio loses 60-70 percent of recruiter views because they will not email you for the password.

Do I need to know HTML and CSS as a UX Designer in 2026?

Not required at most product companies but helpful for collaboration with engineering and increasingly expected at startups under 50 people. Basic literacy (you can read a CSS file, understand flex/grid, write a button component) is enough; you do not need to ship production code.

Should I list every project from school or bootcamp?

Only if it is your strongest work and you have fewer than 2 years of paid experience. After your first real product role, school projects clutter the resume; replace them with deeper case studies of shipped work.

How do I handle the AI design tools question on my resume?

Be specific about which AI tools you actually use in your workflow (Figma AI, v0, Galileo, Uizard) and what for (variant exploration, copy iteration, dev handoff). Vague claims of AI-fluent are filler; concrete workflow integration reads credible.
