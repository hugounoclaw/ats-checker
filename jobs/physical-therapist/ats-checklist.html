Physical Therapist ATS Resume Checklist (2026)

12 ATS-resume checks Physical Therapists need to pass in 2026, the keywords recruiters scan for, and three role-specific resume bullets to copy.

Outpatient ortho chains, hospital systems, SNFs, home health agencies, and pediatric clinics all process PT applications through Workday, iCIMS, or specialty platforms (HRSoft, JazzHR), and every one of them filters on DPT degree, NPTE pass, and state PT license number. Compliance gates also check for active CPR/BLS. Missing any of those in a structured field can prevent your file from ever surfacing.

Productivity-driven outpatient employers further filter on EMR (WebPT, Raintree, Prompt, Net Health, Epic Rehab), case-mix experience, and outcomes platforms (FOTO, CareConnections, MIPS reporting). A resume that lists "treated orthopedic patients" without naming setting, EMR, productivity percentage, and outcome platform reads as generic to a hiring DPT screening 60 applications a week.

The 12-point ATS checklist for Physical Therapists

  1. DPT degree, school, year visible at topWrite "Doctor of Physical Therapy (DPT), [University], 2022." The DPT acronym is what parsers match; "physical therapy doctorate" can miss the keyword.
  2. State PT license number and NPTE passInclude "PT License, State of NC, #PT-12345, exp 06/2027" and "NPTE passed [year]." Multistate compact (PT Compact) eligibility should be noted separately.
  3. Specialty certifications spelled with ABPTSUse OCS, SCS, NCS, GCS, PCS, CCS, WCS exactly. Add APTA membership and credentialed clinical instructor (CCI) if applicable — these score for academic and teaching reqs.
  4. Setting clearly labeled per roleOutpatient ortho, acute IP, IRF, SNF, home health, peds, sports, neuro rehab, women's health. Reqs are setting-specific; mislabeling pushes your resume to the wrong queue.
  5. Productivity percentage per roleOutpatient employers measure 75-90% productive. Write "averaged 85% productivity across 60-patient weekly caseload." SNF reqs measure billable units/day; list those instead where applicable.
  6. Patient volume and visit length"12-14 patients/day, 45-min eval / 30-min follow-up" tells a manager your pace. Combined with productivity, it's the most predictive triage signal for outpatient hiring.
  7. EMR named per roleWebPT, Raintree, Prompt, Net Health, Casamba, Epic Rehab, Cerner. EMR familiarity reduces onboarding by weeks; missing the system name forfeits points on reqs that name a specific platform.
  8. Outcomes platform and MIPS familiarityFOTO, CareConnections, MIPS quality measures, PROMIS. Outpatient ortho reqs increasingly require outcomes documentation — name the platform you've used.
  9. Modalities and manual techniquesList dry needling (with cert + state), IASTM, Mulligan, McKenzie, Maitland, Graston, NDT, vestibular, LSVT BIG, manual lymphatic drainage. Modalities map directly to specialty reqs.
  10. CPT codes and documentation evidenceReference therapeutic exercise (97110), neuromuscular re-ed (97112), manual therapy (97140), gait training (97116). Familiarity with the 8-minute rule and MIPS-relevant code combinations matters for billing compliance.
  11. Caseload demographics and case mixOrtho post-op (TKA, THA, RCR, ACL), spine, geriatrics, athletes, work comp, pediatrics. Specialty practices want prior exposure to their case mix; list it explicitly.
  12. Supervision and team-lead experiencePTA supervision ratios, student CI hours, lead-clinician roles, mentoring junior staff. Clinic-director track reqs filter for this evidence specifically.

Common ATS rejection reasons for Physical Therapists

✗ License or NPTE not in structured field
Fix:Add Licensure section with state, number, expiration, and NPTE year on separate lines.
✗ Productivity percentage missing
Fix:Add productivity per outpatient role; outpatient chains screen on this number above almost every other metric.
✗ EMR generic or absent
Fix:Name WebPT, Raintree, Prompt, Net Health, Epic Rehab per role — clinic chains filter on their own system.
✗ Specialty cert written long-form
Fix:Use OCS, SCS, NCS, GCS, WCS exactly — ABPTS acronyms are the ATS-matchable strings.
✗ Setting mismatch (acute resume for outpatient req)
Fix:Rewrite for the target setting: emphasize relevant case mix, productivity model, and EMR; bury irrelevant settings.
✗ No outcomes evidence
Fix:Reference FOTO scores, MIPS reporting, or PROMIS data — outpatient ortho reqs filter on this with increasing frequency.

FAQ — Physical Therapist ATS questions

Do I put my license number on the resume or just the state?

Put both. License number, state, and expiration in a dedicated Licensure block lets credentialing pre-verify against the state board, which shortens the gap between offer and start. Compact eligibility belongs on a separate line so multi-state employers see it.

Is productivity percentage really expected on a PT resume?

For outpatient ortho and pediatric clinic chains, yes — recruiters screen on 80%+ productivity as a baseline. For acute, SNF, and home health, billable units/day or visits/week are the equivalent metric. Pick the metric that matches your setting and put a number on it.

Should I list every modality I've ever used?

List the ones you do weekly and any cert-backed techniques (dry needling, LSVT BIG, vestibular cert, lymphedema). A long modality list without certs reads as inflated; specific named techniques tied to your case mix score better.

Do I list NPTE score?

No. NPTE is pass/fail for licensure purposes and the actual score is not used by employers. State that you passed and which year if you graduated recently; otherwise the active license is the proof.

How do I show MIPS reporting experience?

Add a bullet like "Submitted MIPS quality measures via [registry], achieved [composite score] across 2024 reporting year." Outpatient practices that participate in MIPS look for this language specifically because performance-based reimbursement depends on it.
