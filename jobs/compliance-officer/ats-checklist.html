Compliance Officer ATS Resume Checklist (2026)

12 ATS-resume checks Compliance Officers need to pass in 2026, the keywords recruiters scan for, and three role-specific resume bullets to copy.

Compliance officer resumes are scanned for regulatory framework keywords (AML/BSA, KYC/CDD, OFAC, FATCA, MiFID II), agency names (SEC, FINRA, OCC, FinCEN, FCA, MAS), and policy-authoring credit. Banks, broker-dealers, asset managers, and fintechs all maintain different filter sets, but ATS-friendly compliance resumes always lead with the regulatory regime they have lived under.

Mid-level seekers must show policy ownership, regulatory-exam support, and risk-assessment outcomes. Senior reviewers expect CCO designation history, board reporting cadence, and named regulator interactions. Tooling keywords (Actimize, Mantas/Oracle FCCM, Fenergo, ComplyAdvantage, NICE) accelerate fintech matches. Generic 'ensured compliance' bullets without regulation citations get cut.

The 12-point ATS checklist for Compliance Officers

  1. List regulatory frameworks with section/article numbersWrite 'Bank Secrecy Act (31 CFR 1020), USA PATRIOT Act §326 CIP, OFAC SDN screening, FATCA (IRC §1471-1474), MiFID II Article 17 algorithmic-trading controls' rather than 'AML compliance'.
  2. Name primary regulators by jurisdictionSpecify 'SEC (Reg BI, Form ADV), FINRA (Rule 3110 supervision), OCC, Federal Reserve, FinCEN, FCA (UK SYSC), MAS (Singapore Notice 626)'. Regulator alphabet soup is the keyword density compliance teams scan for.
  3. Show CAMS, CRCM, or CCEP certificationList 'CAMS (ACAMS Anti-Money Laundering Specialist), CRCM (ABA Certified Regulatory Compliance Manager), CCEP (SCCE Certified Compliance and Ethics Professional), CSCP (NSCP Series)'. Certifications are hard filters.
  4. Document policy authorship by nameQuote 'authored 14 enterprise policies: AML Program, OFAC Sanctions, Code of Ethics, Insider Trading, Gifts & Entertainment, Personal Trading' so policy ownership is concrete, not implied.
  5. Show KYC/CDD/EDD program scopeMention 'designed risk-based CDD model for 240K retail and 8K corporate clients, EDD on 1,200 high-risk relationships, periodic refresh cadence 12/24/36 months by risk tier' to prove program depth.
  6. Quantify SAR filings and AML tuningQuote 'reviewed 2,400 alerts monthly in Actimize SAM, escalated 180 cases to investigations, 42 SARs filed in FY24, tuned 6 scenarios reducing false positives 38%'. SAR volume signals real shop work.
  7. Include regulatory exam and audit historyList 'supported OCC continuous monitoring 2022-2024, two FINRA cycle exams (Rule 3110, Reg BI), responded to MAS thematic review on AML in 2023' to prove regulator-facing experience.
  8. Name compliance technology stackSpecify 'Actimize SAM and CTR, Oracle FCCM/Mantas, Fenergo KYC, ComplyAdvantage screening, RDC (Refinitiv World-Check), MetricStream GRC, NICE trade surveillance' so tooling filters fire.
  9. Show training program metricsQuote 'designed annual AML training for 1,800 employees, 99.4% completion within 30 days, role-based modules for front-office, ops, IT' since training ownership is a CCO competency.
  10. Document board and audit-committee reportingMention 'authored quarterly compliance report to Audit Committee, monthly compliance dashboard to ExCo, annual compliance program assessment per FFIEC Exam Manual' to prove governance fluency.
  11. Include risk assessment frameworks usedList 'FFIEC BSA/AML Examination Manual, COSO ERM, ISO 31000; conducted annual enterprise AML risk assessment with 14 inherent-risk factors and control-effectiveness scoring'.
  12. Show enforcement-action remediation if anyNote 'remediated AML lookback covering 18 months and 92K transactions per FinCEN consent order, completed within agreed 9-month timeline' since remediation experience is rare and valuable.

✗ No CAMS, CRCM, or equivalent certification listed
Fix:Add 'CAMS, ACAMS #123456 (issued 2022, current CE)' or note 'CAMS exam scheduled' on a credentials line.
✗ Surveillance/screening tool not named
Fix:List 'NICE Actimize SAM, OFAC screening via ComplyAdvantage and World-Check, NICE Communication Surveillance for voice and chat' so fintech and bank ATS hit.
✗ SAR work mentioned without volume
Fix:Quote '180 alerts/month reviewed, 42 SARs filed FY24, average filing within 14 days of detection' so investigators can rank throughput.

Fix:Add 'sole author of AML Program (62 pages), Code of Ethics (28 pages), updated annually with board approval' so authorship is verifiable.

FAQ — Compliance Officer ATS questions

How do I list SAR filings without revealing client details?

Quote counts, typologies, and outcomes: '42 SARs filed FY24 across structuring, rapid-movement, and trade-based money laundering typologies; 6 led to law-enforcement information requests.' SAR existence and counts are firm-level metadata; specific subjects are 314(b) protected and never resume content.

Is CAMS or CRCM more valuable for a generalist compliance role?

CAMS is the broader AML credential and recognized globally; CRCM is the deeper US-bank-regulatory credential (Reg E, Reg Z, Reg O, fair lending). Banks weight CRCM; fintechs, broker-dealers, and crypto firms weight CAMS. Many senior compliance professionals hold both; if forced to pick, start with CAMS.

Do I need to mention specific regulators by name?

Yes. Recruiters and search firms filter on 'OCC,' 'FINRA cycle exam,' 'SEC sweep,' 'FCA s.166' literally. Naming the specific regulator and the type of interaction (cycle exam, sweep, MRA response, consent order) tells reviewers exactly what depth of regulator-facing work you have done.

How do I describe enforcement-action work if my employer settled?

Use public-record language. Consent orders and settlement agreements are public; you can describe the matter at the same level as the published order, plus your remediation role: 'remediated lookback per [Order #2023-XXX], completed within 9 months, zero further regulatory criticism.' Avoid disclosing non-public investigation details.

Should I list MiFID II or other EU regulations on a US-only resume?

Only if you actually worked under them. EU-regulation keywords (MiFID II, MAR, GDPR Article 30) accelerate matches for global banks and asset managers with EU operations. Listing them without genuine experience invites failed regulatory-knowledge interview questions and damages credibility for the rest of the screen.
