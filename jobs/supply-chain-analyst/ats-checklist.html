Supply Chain Analyst ATS Resume Checklist (2026)

12 ATS-resume checks Supply Chain Analysts need to pass in 2026, the keywords recruiters scan for, and three role-specific resume bullets to copy.

Supply Chain Analyst resumes are screened on ERP fluency, planning vocabulary, and quantified inventory or service-level improvements. ATS systems scan for SAP (especially S/4HANA, APO, IBP), Oracle, NetSuite, and planning tools (Kinaxis RapidResponse, o9, Blue Yonder, Anaplan). Hiring managers — usually a planning or operations director — look for S&OP experience, OTIF and fill-rate movement, and inventory-turn improvements.

The profession bifurcated post-pandemic. Strategic planning roles want demand-planning, S&OP, and scenario-modeling experience; operational roles want OTIF, freight, 3PL management, and warehouse coordination. Strong resumes match the lean, quantify both cost saves and service improvements, and show analytical depth (Excel power-user, SQL, Python) beyond running standard reports.

The 12-point ATS checklist for Supply Chain Analysts

  1. Name ERP system and version explicitlySAP S/4HANA, SAP ECC, Oracle Fusion, NetSuite, Microsoft Dynamics 365. Module specificity matters — MM, PP, SD, IBP, APO. "SAP" alone is too vague; recruiters filter on module match.
  2. List planning platform if used (Kinaxis, o9, Blue Yonder)These advanced planning systems are differentiators. Even 6 months of Kinaxis RapidResponse, o9, Blue Yonder, Anaplan, or OMP experience opens senior roles closed to ERP-only candidates.
  3. Quantify OTIF or fill-rate improvementsOn-Time-In-Full and fill rate are universal supply KPIs. "Raised OTIF from 87% to 94% across 1,200 SKUs" tells a planning manager exactly what you can do.
  4. Show inventory-turn or working-capital improvementsInventory turns, days of inventory on hand (DIOH), or working capital reduction. "Improved turns 4.2 → 6.1, releasing $4M working capital" is direct CFO-level impact.
  5. Include S&OP cadence and stakeholder involvementSales & Operations Planning cycle — monthly cadence, demand review, supply review, executive S&OP. Name your role (analyst, facilitator) and the stakeholders coordinated.
  6. Demonstrate demand-forecasting methodStatistical forecasting (exponential smoothing, ARIMA, Croston for intermittent), machine-learning models, consensus forecasting. Forecast accuracy (MAPE, WAPE) is the right metric.
  7. Show freight, 3PL, or carrier-management exposureTMS systems (Manhattan, BluJay, MercuryGate), carrier scorecards, freight-spend management. Even adjacent exposure matters for many supply-chain analyst roles.
  8. Reference Six Sigma, Lean, or process improvementGreen Belt or Black Belt certification, DMAIC projects, Kaizen events, value stream mapping. Process-improvement credentials weighted heavily in CPG and manufacturing.
  9. Show Excel power-user depth, plus SQL or PythonAdvanced Excel (Power Query, Power Pivot, VBA) is baseline. SQL for warehouse pulls and Python for forecasting or scenario modeling are differentiators. Name specific use cases.
  10. Include cost-saving initiatives quantified in dollarsNegotiated freight savings, inventory write-down reduction, expedite-cost reduction. "$680K annual freight savings via lane optimization" lands hard.
  11. Reference scenario modeling or what-if analysisNetwork optimization, scenario planning for tariff changes, supplier diversification analysis. Especially relevant in 2026 given geopolitical and tariff volatility; mention scenarios run.
  12. Show cross-functional partnership (sales, ops, finance)S&OP fundamentally cross-functional. Name partners — sales for forecast accuracy, ops for execution, finance for working capital. Pure-analytics resumes lose to operator-analysts.

Common ATS rejection reasons for Supply Chain Analysts

✗ ERP listed without module or version
Fix:Specify SAP S/4HANA + module (MM, PP, IBP) or Oracle Fusion + module. Module match is a hard filter for many supply-chain teams.
✗ No quantified OTIF, fill rate, or inventory turns
Fix:Add at least one supply KPI movement per role. Pure activity descriptions without service or working-capital impact don't pass senior screens.
✗ Pure Excel resume without SQL or Python
Fix:Add SQL for warehouse pulls (even basic) and one Python or Power BI use case. 2026 analyst roles assume beyond-Excel analytical capability.
✗ S&OP claimed without cadence or stakeholders
Fix:Describe the monthly cycle, your role in it, and who you coordinated with. S&OP without operational detail reads as vocabulary recognition.
✗ No Six Sigma or process improvement reference
Fix:If you've done DMAIC or Kaizen work, list it. If not, add a Green Belt as a 2026 development goal. CPG and manufacturing-heavy employers weight it strongly.
✗ Cost savings without dollar figures
Fix:Translate process improvements into dollar saves — freight, inventory write-down, expedite cost. CFOs and supply VPs read in dollars.

FAQ — Supply Chain Analyst ATS questions

Is SAP experience required for most supply chain roles?

Required for most enterprise CPG, manufacturing, and pharma roles. SMB and digitally-native brands often run NetSuite, Oracle NetSuite, or custom stacks. Check 10 target job postings; if SAP dominates, invest in S/4HANA familiarity. Even self-study certifications open more interviews than no SAP exposure.

Should I list Six Sigma Yellow Belt or wait for Green Belt?

List Green Belt and above; Yellow Belt is now closer to participation credit than credential. If you're working toward Green Belt, list it as in-progress with target completion date. CPG and pharma employers weight Green Belt as a meaningful filter; Yellow Belt rarely moves the screen.

Do I need to know Python for supply chain analyst roles in 2026?

Not required, but increasingly differentiating. Python for forecasting (Prophet, statsmodels), scenario modeling, or scraping carrier rates opens senior analyst and analytics-engineer-style supply roles. SQL plus advanced Excel remains the baseline; Python is the lever for stepping up.

How do I show impact when I'm one of many analysts on a team?

Quantify your specific contributions — SKUs owned, DCs covered, forecast accuracy on your portfolio, projects led. "Owned demand planning for 600 SKUs in Western region; improved WAPE 12 points" attributes clearly. Team-level results without personal contribution read as inflated.

Are tariff and geopolitical-risk modeling skills worth highlighting?

Yes in 2026. Tariff volatility, nearshoring shifts, and supplier-diversification analysis are now common analyst asks. If you've run scenarios, modeled tariff impact, or supported sourcing decisions under geopolitical pressure, surface it explicitly — it's a 2026-relevant differentiator.
