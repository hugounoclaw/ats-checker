HR Generalist ATS Resume Checklist (2026)

12 ATS-resume checks HR Generalists need to pass in 2026, the keywords recruiters scan for, and three role-specific resume bullets to copy.

HR Generalist resumes get filtered for HRIS proficiency, employee-relations case volume, and compliance literacy before anyone reads your 'passion for people' summary. Workday, BambooHR, ADP Workforce Now, and UKG Pro are the four platforms most ATS scans key on; if your resume doesn't name at least one, you don't pass the initial 30-second screen at most US employers above 200 headcount.

Hiring managers expect specific FLSA, FMLA, ADA, and I-9/E-Verify experience plus the headcount you supported (e.g., '420 EE client group'). PHR or SHRM-CP listed under Certifications is a strong differentiator. Generalists who can show benefits open-enrollment ownership, terminations handled, and onboarding-NPS or time-to-productivity metrics rank well above peers who describe duties without outcomes.

The 12-point ATS checklist for HR Generalists

  1. Name the HRIS by vendor and versionWrite 'Workday HCM', 'BambooHR', 'ADP Workforce Now', or 'UKG Pro' — not 'HRIS system'. Boolean searches by HR recruiters key on the exact product name.
  2. State client-group headcount supportedQuantify the employee population you partnered with (e.g., '650 EE across 4 states'). Generalist roles scale with headcount; this number maps you to junior, mid, or senior tier.
  3. List PHR, SHRM-CP, or SHRM-SCP certificationPlace credentials in the header next to your name and in a dedicated Certifications block. Many enterprise HR job descriptions list these as 'preferred' and Boolean-search the acronym verbatim.
  4. Quantify employee-relations case volumeNumber of ER cases handled per quarter, types (performance, harassment, accommodation), and outcomes. Senior Generalist roles require demonstrated ER bench strength.
  5. Document FMLA, ADA, and leave administrationSpecify number of leaves administered annually, third-party admin used (Sedgwick, Unum), and any interactive-process experience. Compliance literacy is a knockout criterion.
  6. Cover I-9, E-Verify, and audit experienceMention I-9 audits conducted, E-Verify reauthorizations, and any ICE or DOL audit response work. Multi-state employers screen heavily for this.
  7. Detail benefits open-enrollment ownershipEE count enrolled, plan options, broker partnership, and any change-management or comms you led. OE ownership signals you can run a full HR calendar event end-to-end.
  8. Show onboarding metrics, not just processTime-to-productivity, 90-day retention, or new-hire NPS. 'Built onboarding program' is generic; 'improved 90-day retention from 71% to 88%' is screenable.
  9. Reference multi-state compliance footprintList the states you supported and any high-complexity ones (CA, NY, IL, WA). Multi-state experience commands a 15-25% pay premium per BLS and SHRM survey data.
  10. Mention performance-management cycle ownershipAnnual or semi-annual review cycle, calibration sessions facilitated, and any 9-box or talent-review process. Generalists who own the cycle, not just enter data, rank higher.
  11. Quantify terminations and offboarding handledAnnual involuntary terminations supported, severance agreements processed, and unemployment claims defended. ER weight in your profile matters for Senior Generalist roles.
  12. Avoid 'people person' soft-skill fillerATS parsers and recruiters discount unmeasurable soft claims. Replace with specific behavioral results — 'reduced involuntary turnover 6 points' speaks louder than 'great communicator'.

Common ATS rejection reasons for HR Generalists

✗ No HRIS vendor named
Fix:Add Workday, BambooHR, ADP, or UKG by exact product name in both Skills and Experience sections. Generic 'HRIS' fails Boolean filters used by 80%+ of HR recruiters.
✗ PHR or SHRM-CP not listed in header
Fix:Move certs to the header line under your name plus a Certifications section. Many job postings include cert as a 'required' knockout.
✗ Employee-relations work described in general terms
Fix:Quantify ER case volume per quarter and break out case types. 'Handled 40+ ER cases including 8 harassment investigations' is far more screenable than 'managed employee relations'.
✗ Client-group headcount missing
Fix:Add the employee count you supported next to each role. Headcount is the single best proxy for scope in HR Generalist hiring.
✗ Generic 'partnered with leadership' phrasing
Fix:Replace with outcomes — calibration cycles led, manager training hours delivered, attrition deltas. Recruiters discount unmeasurable partnership claims.

Three example resume bullets for a HR Generalist

Patterns a strong HR Generalist bullet should hit: action verb at the start, role-specific noun in the middle, measurable number at the end. Adapt these to your real work; do not copy verbatim.

  • Partnered with 4 business leaders supporting 480 EE client group in Workday HCM; resolved 52 ER cases in FY24 with zero EEOC charges sustained.
  • Owned annual benefits open enrollment for 620 EE across 6 states; achieved 96% on-time election rate and negotiated 4.2% medical-plan increase versus 7.8% market.
  • Administered 88 FMLA and 14 ADA accommodations through Sedgwick; improved leave-return rate to 91% and cut average leave duration 11 days via interactive-process coaching.

FAQ — HR Generalist ATS questions

Do I need PHR or SHRM-CP to land an HR Generalist role?

Not for entry-level Coordinator roles, but yes effectively for Senior Generalist and HRBP roles at most mid-market and enterprise employers. PHR and SHRM-CP both signal the same baseline. SHRM-CP slightly favored at people-ops-style tech employers; PHR favored in traditional industries.

Should I name specific employees or managers in ER bullets?

Never. Describe case type, outcome, and dollar/time impact in aggregate. 'Resolved 12 performance-improvement-plan cases with 7 successful turnarounds' protects confidentiality and still scores.

How granular should I be about benefit plans I administered?

List carriers (Aetna, Cigna, Kaiser), broker relationship, and plan types (PPO, HDHP, FSA, HSA, 401k vendor). This depth signals genuine benefits ownership rather than just enrollment-support work.

Is multi-state compliance worth calling out?

Absolutely. List specific states and complexity flags (CA wage statements, NYC pay transparency, IL paid leave). Multi-state Generalists are scarce and command premium offers.

Can I list employee count even if I was on a larger HR team?

Yes — clarify your individual client group within the team. Example: 'HR Generalist on 6-person team supporting 2,400 EE; primary HRBP for 540 EE in Sales and Marketing.'
