Paralegal ATS Resume Checklist (2026)

12 ATS-resume checks Paralegals need to pass in 2026, the keywords recruiters scan for, and three role-specific resume bullets to copy.

Paralegal resumes are filtered by legal staffing teams and law firm recruiting coordinators for practice area, case-management software, eDiscovery tools, and billable-hour expectations. Generic 'legal experience' resumes lose to specialists who lead with practice area (commercial litigation, IP prosecution, immigration, real estate, M&A) and the case-management platform they ran daily — Clio, MyCase, Smokeball, NetDocuments, iManage, or Worldox.

Litigation paralegals must show Relativity, Concordance, or Everlaw experience for eDiscovery, plus Westlaw and LexisNexis research depth. Corporate paralegals lean on M&A diligence-room platforms (Intralinks, Datasite). NALA or NFPA certification, ABA-approved program completion, billable hour expectations met, and Bates-numbered production volume distinguish working paralegals from administrative legal support.

The 12-point ATS checklist for Paralegals

  1. State practice area clearlyCommercial litigation, IP, M&A, immigration, real estate, employment, family law. Practice-area specificity is the #1 filter — generalist resumes lose to specialists in every hiring funnel.
  2. Name case-management software by vendorClio, MyCase, Smokeball, PracticePanther, iManage Work, NetDocuments, Worldox. ATS and recruiter searches Boolean-match the platform name.
  3. List eDiscovery tools for litigationRelativity (and certification level), Everlaw, Concordance, Disco, Logikcull. Doc-review volume in pages or GB processed if substantial.
  4. Show Westlaw and LexisNexis depthYears of use, specific products (Westlaw Edge, Lexis+, Practical Law), and research-memo output volume. Research depth differentiates litigation paralegals.
  5. List NALA, NFPA, or state certificationNALA Certified Paralegal (CP), Advanced Certified Paralegal (ACP), NFPA PACE/RP, or state-bar paralegal certifications (CA, TX, FL). Place in header credentials.
  6. Quantify billable hours expectation metAnnual billable hours billed (e.g., '1,650 billable hours in FY24'). Paralegals at large firms have billable targets; meeting them is core proof of throughput.
  7. Show ABA-approved paralegal programMany firms require or strongly prefer ABA-approved program graduation. Include institution and program type (certificate vs associate vs bachelor's).
  8. Document trial-preparation experienceTrials supported, witness binders prepared, exhibit lists assembled, trial-tech support (TrialDirector, OnCue). Trial bench is a litigation-paralegal differentiator.
  9. Detail Bates numbering and production volumeDocuments Bates-stamped and produced (e.g., '180,000+ pages produced across 4 productions'). Volume signals real litigation-support depth.
  10. Cover state and federal court e-filingPACER (CM/ECF), state e-filing systems (NYSCEF, eFileNV, Odyssey). Filing fluency is a daily-use skill in litigation.
  11. List law firm tier or in-house contextAm Law 100/200 firm, mid-market firm, boutique, Fortune 500 in-house. Setting tier maps to pay band and workflow expectations.
  12. Avoid 'detail-oriented' soft-skill fillerEvery paralegal claim is 'detail-oriented'. Replace with specific volume, accuracy, or outcome — production counts, deposition summaries, motion-prep hours.

Common ATS rejection reasons for Paralegals

✗ No practice area specified
Fix:Lead with commercial litigation, IP, M&A, immigration, or your true specialty. Generalist framing loses to specialist resumes in every law firm hiring funnel.
✗ Case-management software not named
Fix:Add Clio, iManage, NetDocuments, Smokeball, or whatever you ran daily. Generic 'case management software' fails recruiter Boolean filters.
✗ eDiscovery tools missing for litigation roles
Fix:Add Relativity (with certification if held), Everlaw, or Concordance. Litigation roles screen heavily for eDiscovery platform fluency.
✗ No certification (NALA, NFPA, state)
Fix:Pursue NALA CP if not held — it's the most widely recognized US paralegal certification and appears in many JD 'preferred' lists.
✗ Billable hours and case volume missing
Fix:Add annual billable hours, depositions summarized, productions processed. Volume metrics prove throughput beyond title alone.

FAQ — Paralegal ATS questions

Do I list the cases I worked on or is that a confidentiality issue?

Describe matter type, dollar value if public or rounded, and your specific role without naming clients or case captions (unless public record and not confidential). 'Supported $40M trade-secret litigation in S.D.N.Y.' is appropriate; naming the parties typically is not. When in doubt, ask your supervising attorney.

Is NALA Certified Paralegal worth pursuing?

Yes for most paralegals serious about career growth. NALA CP is the most widely recognized US certification and appears in many job descriptions as preferred or required. The exam covers ethics, substantive law, and skills; the credential earns higher starting offers at most firms.

Should I include the law school I attended if I went but didn't finish?

List the J.D. program and credits completed if substantial (e.g., '2L year completed, withdrew for personal reasons'). It demonstrates legal-doctrinal depth beyond paralegal training and explains research and writing strength.

How do I show eDiscovery depth without Relativity certification?

Quantify document review volume (pages, GBs), platforms used (Everlaw, Concordance, Disco), and any review-team leadership. Working volume in any platform is more meaningful than a single certification.

Do in-house paralegal roles use the same resume as law-firm roles?

Largely yes, but emphasize contract-management software (Ironclad, Agiloft, ContractWorks), board-meeting and entity-management work (Diligent, Athennian), and any compliance program support. In-house roles prize cross-functional partnership; firm roles prize matter throughput and billable discipline.
