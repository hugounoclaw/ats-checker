Registered Nurse ATS Resume Checklist (2026)

12 ATS-resume checks Registered Nurses need to pass in 2026, the keywords recruiters scan for, and three role-specific resume bullets to copy.

Hospital systems run RN applications through Workday, iCIMS, or Taleo with parsers tuned to extract license number, license state, and specialty certifications before a recruiter ever sees the file. If your RN license string is buried in a sentence ("licensed in NJ since 2019") instead of a structured field, the requisition's compliance gate may auto-flag you as missing credentials even when you have them.

Magnet hospitals and large IDNs also score for unit-specific experience. "ICU" alone is weaker than "24-bed mixed MICU/SICU, 2:1 ratio, Epic ICU module." Cert acronyms (CCRN, PCCN, CEN, OCN) must appear exactly as ANCC/AACN writes them, with expiration year nearby, because credential-verification bots cross-check.

The 12-point ATS checklist for Registered Nurses

  1. RN license line with state and numberPut a dedicated Licensure section: "RN, State of Texas, License #123456, exp 04/2027." Workday and iCIMS parsers extract this as a structured field; prose burial causes compliance auto-rejects.
  2. BSN, ADN, or MSN spelled and abbreviatedWrite "Bachelor of Science in Nursing (BSN)" once so both keyword and acronym scans match. Magnet hospitals filter for BSN minimum; ADN-only candidates should add "BSN in progress, exp 2027" if true.
  3. Specialty certs in ANCC/AACN exact formatUse CCRN, PCCN, CEN, OCN, CNOR, RNC-OB, CMSRN exactly as the credentialing body writes them. Avoid "Critical Care Certified" — ATS does string match on the acronym, not the description.
  4. BLS, ACLS, PALS with AHA and expirationList "BLS (AHA), exp 08/2026" and the same for ACLS/PALS/NRP. Expired cards on a resume trigger automatic disqualification at most hospital systems; missing dates trigger recruiter follow-up delay.
  5. Unit type and bed count per jobReplace "ICU nurse" with "24-bed CVICU, 2:1 ratio, 600-bed Level I trauma center." Unit acronym (MICU, SICU, NICU, PICU, ED, L&D, PACU, Tele) is a hard ATS keyword in specialty reqs.
  6. EHR system named in each roleList Epic (and modules like ASAP, Stork, ClinDoc), Cerner PowerChart, Meditech, or Allscripts. Travel and float pool reqs filter on Epic specifically; "electronic health records" is too generic for the parser.
  7. Patient ratio and shift typeInclude "1:4 med-surg nights, 12-hour shifts" or "1:2 ICU days." Hiring managers triage resumes by acuity — a 1:6 telemetry nurse and a 1:1 ECMO nurse look identical without the ratio.
  8. Procedures and skills inventory sectionList central line care, vent management, CRRT, chemo administration, conscious sedation, code response, IV starts/hr. Unit-specific reqs scan for these skill verbs; recruiters use them to map you to open positions.
  9. Magnet, teaching, or trauma-level contextNote "Magnet-designated 800-bed academic medical center" or "Level II trauma, community hospital." Acuity context lets recruiters at peer institutions place you accurately and short-circuits the screening call.
  10. Compact license states listed separatelyIf you hold an eNLC multistate license, write "RN Compact (eNLC), home state TN." Travel agencies and multi-state IDNs filter for compact specifically and reject single-state nurses for cross-border roles.
  11. Outcome metrics, not just dutiesQuantify CAUTI/CLABSI reductions, HCAHPS scores, fall rates, readmission percentages, preceptor count. Hospitals running value-based-care reqs (CMS, ANCC) score these explicitly above task lists.
  12. Continuing education and committeesList shared governance, unit council, charge nurse rotations, CE hours toward license renewal, ANA membership. Magnet applications and clinical-ladder promotions require this evidence; recruiters know it.

Common ATS rejection reasons for Registered Nurses

✗ License number missing or unparseable
Fix:Add a Licensure section at top with state, license number, and expiration on a single line — not embedded in prose.
✗ BLS/ACLS expired or undated
Fix:Include issuing body (AHA) and expiration month/year for every life-support card; renew before applying if within 60 days.
✗ Specialty cert written long-form, not as acronym
Fix:Use exact strings CCRN, PCCN, CEN, OCN — ATS does literal match. Put the acronym first, definition in parens after.
✗ Unit type unclear (just says "floor nurse")
Fix:Name the unit (MICU, step-down, oncology, L&D) and include bed count plus typical patient ratio for that role.
✗ EHR not listed by name
Fix:Spell out Epic (with modules), Cerner, Meditech, or Allscripts in each role — most travel and acute-care reqs require named system experience.
✗ No outcome metrics, only task verbs
Fix:Add HCAHPS, CAUTI/CLABSI, fall rate, or readmission numbers — Magnet and value-based-care reqs filter on these.

FAQ — Registered Nurse ATS questions

Do I put my license number on the resume or just the state?

Put both. Compliance-driven hospital ATS (Workday, iCIMS) often parses a Licensure block into a structured credentialing field, and recruiters can pre-verify you against Nursys before calling. State alone causes a follow-up email that adds days to the pipeline.

Do recruiters care if I'm ADN versus BSN?

Magnet and most academic medical centers filter for BSN; some require BSN within 2-5 years of hire. List BSN if you have it, and if you are ADN-only with active enrollment, write "RN-to-BSN, [School], exp [year]" so the filter still sees the keyword.

Should I list every CEU or just the certifications?

List active certifications (CCRN, OCN, BLS, ACLS) with expiration, and group CEUs only if they map to your target unit (chemotherapy provider card for an oncology role, for example). Generic CEU lists pad the resume without changing match scores.

How do I show travel-nurse contracts without looking job-hoppy?

Group them under one "Travel Nurse — [Agency]" header and list each contract as a sub-bullet with hospital name, unit, dates, and EHR. Recruiters expect 13-week assignments in travel — the format signals it instead of looking like turnover.

Do I need to include my GPA or NCLEX score?

No on NCLEX (it's pass/fail and verifiable). Include GPA only if you graduated within 2 years and it is 3.5+. After that, clinical experience outranks academic numbers in every nursing ATS scoring model.
