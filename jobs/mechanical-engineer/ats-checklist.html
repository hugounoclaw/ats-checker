Mechanical Engineer ATS Resume Checklist (2026)

12 ATS-resume checks Mechanical Engineers need to pass in 2026, the keywords recruiters scan for, and three role-specific resume bullets to copy.

Mechanical engineering resumes are scanned for CAD platform, FEA fluency, GD&T literacy, and manufacturing-process exposure. The same resume looks very different to an aerospace recruiter (CATIA, Abaqus, AS9100) versus a consumer-products recruiter (SolidWorks, injection molding, DFM). Tailoring the tool stack to the target industry is half the battle.

Mid-level mechanical engineers need to quantify part count, BOM cost, tolerance stack analysis, and at least one product transferred to volume manufacturing. Senior reviewers look for ASME Y14.5 GD&T usage, PE licensure where relevant (HVAC, building systems, structural), and proof you wrote real DFM reports that changed tooling decisions. Generic 'designed parts' bullets get cut in the first pass.

The 12-point ATS checklist for Mechanical Engineers

  1. Name CAD platform with version and assembly sizeSpecify 'SolidWorks 2024 (top-down assembly, 1,200 parts), CATIA V5 R30, NX 2206' rather than 'CAD software'. Recruiters filter by exact platform and the complexity of assemblies you owned.
  2. List FEA solver, mesh type, and validation methodQuantify 'ANSYS Mechanical 2023R2, second-order tetrahedral mesh, validated against strain-gauge testing within 8%' to show the FEA was real engineering, not auto-meshed colored pictures.
  3. Show ASME Y14.5 GD&T applicationMention 'GD&T per ASME Y14.5-2018, datum reference frames, positional tolerance with MMC' on critical drawings. Hiring managers reject candidates whose drawings only carry plus-minus dimensions.
  4. Declare PE license or FE/EIT progressWrite 'Professional Engineer (PE), Mechanical, Ohio #PE-67890' or 'EIT, FE Mechanical passed Oct 2023'. Building-systems, HVAC, and pressure-vessel roles often require PE on the stamp.
  5. List manufacturing processes you DFM-reviewedSpecify 'injection molding (gating, draft, sink), CNC milling (3-axis and 5-axis), sheet metal (bend reliefs, K-factor 0.42), die casting' instead of 'familiar with manufacturing'. Process-specific DFM is what NPI teams hire for.
  6. Quantify tolerance stack analysisWrite '1D worst-case and RSS stack on 14-part bearing assembly, hit 0.05mm gap target with 1.33 Cpk projection' so reviewers see you used statistical methods, not just sums.
  7. Show BOM ownership and cost-down outcomesMention 'owned BOM in Arena PLM, drove 18% material cost reduction by switching from 6061-T6 billet to A380 die cast'. BOM and cost-down moves are senior-level signal.
  8. List materials and heat-treatment specificationsSpecify 'Inconel 718 (AMS 5662), 17-4PH H900, 6061-T6 anodized per MIL-A-8625 Type II Class 2' to prove material literacy beyond 'aluminum and steel'.
  9. Call out standards: ASME, ISO, AS9100, IATF 16949Name 'ASME BPVC Section VIII Div 1, ISO 9001:2015, AS9100D, IATF 16949' relevant to your industry. ATS for regulated mechanical roles is built from these standard numbers.
  10. Include simulation beyond static FEAMention 'modal analysis, nonlinear contact, transient thermal, CFD in Star-CCM+ or Fluent' if you ran them. Linear static alone signals junior; nonlinear and multiphysics signal senior.
  11. Document prototype-to-production transitionsWrite 'transferred 3 designs from SLA prototype to MUD-base 32-cavity tool, supported PPAP Level 3 with supplier in Suzhou'. NPI/transfer evidence is rare and highly valued.
  12. Show test plan authorship and DVT outcomesList 'authored DVT plan covering drop (1.2m per IEC 60068-2-31), thermal cycle (-40 to 85C), vibration (random 7.7 Grms per MIL-STD-810G)' so reliability teams see real test engineering.

Common ATS rejection reasons for Mechanical Engineers

✗ CAD tool listed without version or assembly complexity
Fix:Replace 'SolidWorks' with 'SolidWorks 2024, top-down assembly 850 parts, in-context modeling and configuration tables' so recruiters can rank your fluency.
✗ FEA claimed without mesh, BC, or validation evidence
Fix:Add 'ANSYS Workbench, hex-dominant mesh, fixed-support BC, validated within 6% of strain-gauge data' to prove the analysis was defensible.
✗ Drawings described without GD&T per ASME Y14.5
Fix:Note 'released production drawings per ASME Y14.5-2018 with datum schemes and positional tolerance' so QE and supplier teams trust the drafting standard.
✗ No manufacturing process named on shipped parts
Fix:Specify 'design for injection molding, 1.5-degree draft, gate location optimized via Moldflow' so DFM ownership is visible.
✗ PE eligibility hidden inside a paragraph
Fix:Add a clear 'Licensure' line and include exam status (FE passed, PE candidate with 4 years progressive experience) so recruiters can score it instantly.
✗ BOM cost work missing dollar or percentage
Fix:Quote '14% BOM reduction, $3.20 per unit at 250kU/year, $800K annual savings' so the impact is rankable against other candidates.

FAQ — Mechanical Engineer ATS questions

Do I need a PE license for an in-house mechanical design role?

Usually no for product design at consumer or industrial OEMs. PE is required for building systems (HVAC, plumbing, structural), pressure vessels stamped per ASME BPVC, and any work where you sign off on public-safety drawings. Listing FE/EIT shows you are on the path even if PE is not mandatory.

How do I show GD&T fluency if my employer only used plus-minus dimensions?

Take an ASME Y14.5-2018 short course (Tec-Ease, Sigmetrix, ASME e-learning) and add the certificate. Then write at least one bullet showing you applied positional, profile, or runout tolerance to a recent project, even on a side or internal-tool drawing.

Should I list FEA on my resume if I only used SolidWorks Simulation?

Yes, but be specific: 'SolidWorks Simulation, linear static and modal, validated against hand calcs per Roark.' Do not pad with ANSYS or Abaqus unless you ran them. Interviewers test FEA claims with mesh-quality and boundary-condition questions; bluffing fails fast.

How much manufacturing detail is too much for an early-career resume?

Pick two processes you genuinely owned a DFM review on (injection molding and sheet metal are common) and go deep on each: gate location, draft, K-factor, bend reliefs. One deep example beats five shallow ones, and reviewers can quiz you confidently in the interview.

Do I list CFD if I only used built-in SolidWorks Flow Simulation?

Yes, framed accurately: 'SolidWorks Flow Simulation for thermal management of 35W LED driver, correlated within 4C of thermocouple data.' Skip claiming Fluent or Star-CCM+ unless you ran those solvers. CFD interviewers ask about turbulence models and y+ values quickly.
