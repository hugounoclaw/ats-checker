Data Analyst ATS Resume Checklist (2026)

12 ATS-resume checks Data Analysts need to pass in 2026, the keywords recruiters scan for, and three role-specific resume bullets to copy.

Data Analyst resumes hit two gatekeepers before a human reads them: an ATS scanning for SQL dialects, BI tools, and statistical methods, and a hiring manager looking for evidence you've owned a dashboard end-to-end, not just pulled queries on request. Recruiters in 2026 expect to see at least one warehouse (Snowflake, BigQuery, Redshift), one BI surface (Tableau, Looker, Power BI), and modeling work in dbt or equivalent.

The biggest filter is showing analytical judgment, not tool count. Cohort retention curves, funnel drop-off analysis, A/B test reads with confidence intervals, and root-cause investigations all rank higher than "built dashboards." Quantify the decisions your numbers drove — revenue moved, churn reduced, ops hours saved.

The 12-point ATS checklist for Data Analysts

  1. Name SQL dialect and warehouse explicitlyWrite "Snowflake SQL," "BigQuery Standard SQL," or "Postgres" rather than just "SQL." Recruiters filter on dialect because window-function syntax and cost models differ, and the role usually inherits a stack.
  2. List one BI tool as primary, two as secondaryPick Tableau, Looker, or Power BI as your headline tool with version or LookML/DAX depth. Listing five BI tools equally reads as shallow; depth in one beats breadth across all.
  3. Show dbt or transformation layer experiencedbt models, tests, exposures, and macros are 2026 table stakes for analytics engineering-adjacent roles. If you've owned staging or mart models, name the layer and number of models maintained.
  4. Quantify dashboards owned and their audience"Owned 12 executive dashboards consumed weekly by C-suite" beats "built dashboards." Include refresh cadence, user count, and whether you defined the metric layer or inherited it.
  5. Include at least one A/B test with statistical detailName the metric, sample size, test duration, lift, and confidence level. "Designed A/B test on checkout copy, 18K users, +4.2% conversion, p<0.05" signals you read tests, not just dashboards.
  6. Demonstrate Python or R for analysis, not just SQLPandas, NumPy, scikit-learn, or tidyverse signal you can do regression, clustering, or forecasting beyond GROUP BY. Note specific libraries used and what problem they solved.
  7. Mention cohort, funnel, or retention analysis specificallyThese are the three analyses every product/growth team needs. Listing them by name with a business outcome ("identified 30-day retention cliff in onboarding cohort") proves applied skill.
  8. Include version control for analytics workGit for SQL repos, dbt projects, or notebook reviews is now expected on serious analytics teams. "Reviewed pull requests on shared dbt repo" signals collaborative analytics maturity.
  9. Reference stakeholder partnerships by functionNaming the teams you supported — Growth, Finance, CX, Supply — shows you translate business questions to data. Add the cadence (weekly readouts, monthly business reviews).
  10. Show data-quality and documentation ownershipdbt tests, freshness checks, data dictionary contributions, or Looker LookML governance signal you treat data as a product. Mention specific tests written or docs maintained.
  11. List statistical methods you actually appliedRegression, t-tests, chi-square, time-series decomposition, propensity scoring — name only what you've shipped. Recruiters and managers ask follow-up questions; vague claims collapse in interview.
  12. Include a portfolio link with notebooks or write-upsGitHub with cleaned-data case studies, Tableau Public profile, or a blog walking through one analysis. Strong candidates show their reasoning, not just final charts.

Common ATS rejection reasons for Data Analysts

✗ Lists every BI tool with no depth in any
Fix:Pick one primary tool and show calculated fields, LookML, or DAX measures you built. Demote the rest to a secondary line.
✗ Claims A/B testing without a single test detailed
Fix:Add one test with metric, sample, duration, lift, and confidence. Even one well-documented test outweighs three vague mentions.
✗ Only SQL listed, no Python or R
Fix:Add at least one analysis (forecasting, clustering, statistical test) done in pandas, NumPy, or tidyverse. Pure SQL reads as report-builder in 2026.
✗ Dashboard count without business outcomes
Fix:Replace "built 40 dashboards" with "owned 6 dashboards driving weekly revenue reviews; surfaced $1.2M leakage in Q3."
✗ No mention of stakeholder partnership
Fix:Name the partner functions and the rhythm — "weekly readouts with Growth PM," "monthly QBR analysis for Finance." Pure-tech resumes signal order-taker, not analyst.

Three example resume bullets for a Data Analyst

Patterns a strong Data Analyst bullet should hit: action verb at the start, role-specific noun in the middle, measurable number at the end. Adapt these to your real work; do not copy verbatim.

  • Owned 8 executive Looker dashboards (LookML) for mid-market SaaS, $40M ARR; reduced ad-hoc requests 62% by codifying 23 reusable explores and adding tier-based access controls.

  • Built dbt mart layer (32 models, 140+ tests) on Snowflake replacing legacy stored procs; cut weekly finance close from 4 days to 6 hours and eliminated 3 recurring data-quality incidents.

FAQ — Data Analyst ATS questions

Do I list SQL if I only know basic SELECT and JOIN?

Only if you can write a window function and a CTE under interview pressure. Listing SQL with weak fundamentals gets caught in the first technical screen. Instead, write "SQL (intermediate)" and be honest, or invest a week leveling up before applying.

Should I include certifications like Google Data Analytics?

List entry-level certs only if you have under two years of experience and no STEM degree. They signal commitment to career-switchers but become noise on a mid/senior resume. Replace with a portfolio, dbt cloud cert, or specific BI vendor cert if relevant.

How do I show impact if my dashboards are internal and confidential?

Abstract the number, not the metric. "Drove 40% reduction in weekly reporting hours across 60-person ops team" reveals nothing proprietary. Confirm with your employer; most companies allow percentage-based achievements with anonymized context.

Is Excel still worth listing on a data analyst resume?

Yes — but as a complement, not a headline. Senior leadership consumes work in spreadsheets, and pivot tables, INDEX/MATCH, and Power Query remain expected. Listing Excel as your top tool above SQL signals you're applying for a reporting role, not analyst.

Do I need to know machine learning to compete?

Not for most analyst roles in 2026. Statistical literacy, A/B testing, and forecasting matter more than ML titles. If a job posting emphasizes ML, the role is likely data scientist; don't compete there without project evidence.
