Medical Assistant ATS Resume Checklist (2026)

12 ATS-resume checks Medical Assistants need to pass in 2026, the keywords recruiters scan for, and three role-specific resume bullets to copy.

Primary care groups, urgent care chains, and specialty clinics process MA applications through Workday, iCIMS, Greenhouse, or Paychex with parsers that filter on CMA (AAMA), RMA (AMT), CCMA (NHA), or NCMA (NCCT). Most reqs require national certification; uncertified candidates are screened out before a clinic manager sees the file even when clinical skill is strong.

Clinics also filter on EHR experience by name — Athenahealth, eClinicalWorks, Epic, NextGen, Practice Fusion — and on whether you have done back-office clinical work, front-office administrative work, or both. Generic "medical assistant" framing without those specifics looks unqualified next to a resume that names cert, EHR, vaccine admin volume, and ICD-10/CPT coding exposure.

The 12-point ATS checklist for Medical Assistants

  1. MA cert with credentialing body abbreviationWrite "CMA (AAMA), Cert #1234567, exp 05/2027" or "RMA (AMT)," "CCMA (NHA)," "NCMA (NCCT)." The body in parentheses is what the parser matches — "Certified Medical Assistant" alone often fails.
  2. Back-office vs front-office scope labeledSplit duties: clinical (vitals, injections, EKG, phlebotomy, specimen prep) vs administrative (scheduling, insurance verification, prior auth, copay collection). Reqs are often one or the other; clarity moves you into the right pile.
  3. EHR named in every roleList Athenahealth, eClinicalWorks (eCW), Epic, NextGen, Practice Fusion, Cerner, Allscripts, DrChrono. EHR is a top-3 keyword on MA reqs; the parser scores by exact system name.
  4. Vaccine and injection administrationSpecify IM, subQ, intradermal; pediatric immunization schedule (VFC if applicable); flu/COVID/Tdap volume per season. Pediatric and primary-care reqs filter on this.
  5. Phlebotomy and specimen processingNote venipuncture, capillary, urine, throat/nasal swabs, strep/flu/COVID rapid testing, specimen labeling, and lab courier handoff. Doubles the reqs you qualify for, especially urgent care.
  6. Vital signs and screening volumeQuantify "40-50 patients/day rooming, vitals + history," or specialty-specific (vision/hearing screens, pediatric growth charts, AAA screens). Patient throughput matters in primary care reqs.
  7. ICD-10 and CPT coding exposureIf you assist with charge entry, prior auth, or scribing, write "ICD-10 and CPT-4 entry, prior auth submission via CoverMyMeds." Specialty practices weight this heavily.
  8. Scribing or AVS documentationNote scribing for provider visits and after-visit summary completion. Many MAs functioning as scribes don't list it, missing reqs that explicitly want scribe-trained candidates.
  9. Specialty exposure namedPediatrics, OB/GYN, derm, cardiology, ortho, GI, oncology, behavioral health. Specialty groups filter for prior specialty MA experience because procedure and prep workflows differ.
  10. CPR/BLS card current with AHAList "BLS (AHA), exp 09/2026." Clinics with same-day surgery, sedation, or pediatric reqs make BLS mandatory; expired or undated cards trigger soft reject.
  11. OSHA, HIPAA, and bloodborne trainingAnnual training current — compliance teams pre-screen for these and onboarding speeds up when they're documented on the resume.
  12. Bilingual ability called outSpanish, Vietnamese, Mandarin, ASL, Tagalog, etc. Community clinics and FQHCs often post bilingual-required reqs that auto-filter monolingual applicants.

Common ATS rejection reasons for Medical Assistants

✗ No national cert listed
Fix:Add CMA, RMA, CCMA, or NCMA with issuing body and expiration; most reqs hard-filter on national cert.
✗ EHR not named
Fix:List the actual EHR per role — Athena, eCW, Epic, NextGen. Clinics filter for their own system.
✗ Resume mixes clinical and admin without separating
Fix:Use sub-bullets or sections labeled Clinical and Administrative so back-office-only reqs can score you cleanly.
✗ No volume numbers (patients roomed, vaccines given)
Fix:Add patients/day, injections/season, EKGs/week — throughput is how primary care groups triage.
✗ Specialty experience implied but not stated
Fix:Name the specialty (peds, OB, derm, cardiology) explicitly per role; specialty practices filter for prior specialty exposure.

FAQ — Medical Assistant ATS questions

Do I need national certification to get hired as an MA?

Most corporate primary care and urgent care chains require CMA (AAMA), RMA (AMT), CCMA (NHA), or NCMA (NCCT). Independent practices sometimes hire uncertified candidates with strong experience, but the certified pool gets through ATS filters first. Get certified before applying to large systems.

Should I list every EHR I've touched?

List the ones you used regularly for at least a month, with the role. "Epic (3 years, primary care)," "eClinicalWorks (1 year, dermatology)." A long unattributed EHR list looks padded; named, time-bounded experience scores better.

How do I show I can scribe without a separate scribe credential?

Add "Scribed provider visits in real time, completed AVS documentation, queued orders for provider co-sign" as a bullet under the relevant role. Practices hiring MA-scribes look for this exact workflow language.

Is CMA (AAMA) really better than RMA or CCMA?

CMA (AAMA) is the AAMA credential and is the most-named cert in job descriptions, but RMA, CCMA, and NCMA satisfy almost every requirement. The one trap: a few employers (and some Medicare incident-to billing interpretations) prefer CMA specifically. Check the req.

Do I list externship hours if I just graduated?

Yes — write "160-hour MA externship, [Practice], roomed 200+ patients, administered 80+ injections, documented in eClinicalWorks." Externship without specifics reads as observation; with numbers, it reads as entry-level experience.
