Financial Advisor ATS Resume Checklist (2026)

12 ATS-resume checks Financial Advisors need to pass in 2026, the keywords recruiters scan for, and three role-specific resume bullets to copy.

Financial advisor resumes are filtered first by licensure (Series 7, 63, 65, 66) and designations (CFP, ChFC, CFA), then by book metrics (AUM, household count, retention rate). Wirehouse, RIA, and independent BD recruiters search for exact license combinations and the platform (Salesforce Financial Services Cloud, eMoney, MoneyGuidePro, RightCapital, Black Diamond, Orion). Listing 'investment advice' without license numbers and AUM is a fast rejection.

Mid-career advisors must show fee-only versus commission mix, fiduciary status under Reg BI or 1940 Act, and household-level metrics. Senior reviewers want net new asset growth, client retention, and team production credit. Compliance fluency around suitability, Form CRS, and ADV Part 2 is increasingly required even for non-CCO advisors.

The 12-point ATS checklist for Financial Advisors

  1. List FINRA licenses with CRD and registration statusWrite 'Series 7 (General Securities Rep), Series 66 (Uniform Combined State Law), CRD #1234567, active'. Recruiters filter by exact series and CRD lookup; abbreviations alone get missed.
  2. Declare CFP or ChFC with date and renewal statusSpecify 'CFP Certificant, 2020, active and compliant with 30-hr CE biennial requirement' or 'ChFC, American College, 2019'. Designations are a hard filter for fee-only firms.
  3. Quantify AUM with household and account countQuote 'managed $128M AUM across 142 households, 380 client accounts, average household $900K' rather than 'managed client portfolios'. AUM plus household count proves true book ownership.
  4. Show fee model and revenue mixSpecify 'fee-only RIA, 1.00% on first $1M tiered, no commission revenue' or 'hybrid: 60% advisory fee, 40% transactional, $480K T-12 production'. Comp model is a tight filter.
  5. Declare fiduciary status and registrationMention 'IAR registered under §1940 Investment Advisers Act, Form ADV Part 2A and 2B filed annually, Reg BI compliant with documented best-interest review process'. Fiduciary clarity matters more every year.
  6. Name planning software and CRM platformList 'eMoney Advisor (Decision Center, Heatmap), MoneyGuidePro Elite, RightCapital, Salesforce Financial Services Cloud, Redtail CRM, Orion portfolio accounting'. Platform match accelerates onboarding.
  7. Show client retention and net new assetsQuote '97.8% client retention 3-year rolling, $14.2M NNA in 2024, 18 new households YTD' since these are the metrics partners use to value books.
  8. List investment platforms and custodiansMention 'Schwab Advisor Services, Fidelity Institutional, Pershing, custodial transitions completed for 38 households during 2023 reorganization'. Custodian fluency speeds transitions.
  9. Include planning specialtiesSpecify 'retirement income planning, equity-comp planning (RSU, ISO, NSO, 10b5-1), estate planning coordination with attorneys, tax-loss harvesting in taxable accounts' to differentiate from order-takers.
  10. Show insurance and annuity production if applicableList 'life and health licensed (resident NY, non-resident in 14 states), $2.8M of life insurance face amount placed FY24, fixed and indexed annuity rollover work via 1035 exchange' since insurance fluency expands the book.
  11. Quantify discovery-to-close cycleMention '52 prospect meetings in 2024, 28 converted to managed accounts (54% close), $6.4M new assets at close, 90-day onboarding playbook' since BD metrics matter to recruiting managers.
  12. Document compliance and U4 statusState 'clean U4, zero customer complaints, zero disclosures' clearly. Wirehouse and RIA compliance teams reject candidates with undisclosed Forms U4 events.

FAQ — Financial Advisor ATS questions

Do I list AUM if I am a junior advisor working under a senior?

Yes, but credit clearly. Write 'supported $240M household book, owned client relationships for $42M sub-book across 38 households.' Padding total team AUM as personally managed is a fireable misrepresentation under most BD policies and will surface in reference checks.

Should I list Series 6 if I now have Series 7?

No. Series 7 supersedes Series 6 in coverage. Listing Series 6 alongside Series 7 wastes line space and signals you are unsure which is the operative license. Always list the highest-level qualifying license and any state law license (63, 65, or 66) you maintain.

How do I show fiduciary commitment if I am at a wirehouse?

Document IAR registration on the firm's RIA arm, Form ADV Part 2B brochure supplement, and Reg BI process adherence. Wirehouses operate dual-registrant models, so you can credibly describe fiduciary work for advisory accounts even as a brokerage employee.

Is CFP worth pursuing if I already hold Series 7 and 66?

Yes for nearly every advisory career path. CFP is the most recognized planning credential among consumers and the strongest comp-driving designation in fee-based practices. Series 7 and 66 are entry-level baseline; CFP differentiates you in lead-flow and referral networks.

How do I describe my book if I am leaving and cannot solicit clients?

Describe scope and metrics without naming clients: 'managed $96M AUM, 88 households, 64% retirement-income focus.' Many non-solicits restrict client contact, not resume descriptions. Have employment counsel review your specific Protocol-for-Broker-Recruiting or non-solicit terms before discussing with prospective firms.
