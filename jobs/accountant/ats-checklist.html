Accountant ATS Resume Checklist (2026)

12 ATS-resume checks Accountants need to pass in 2026, the keywords recruiters scan for, and three role-specific resume bullets to copy.

Accounting resumes get hit hardest by ATS keyword filters built around GAAP language, ERP system names, and CPA exam status. Recruiters at public firms and corporate accounting departments search for exact phrases like 'month-end close,' 'journal entries,' 'GL reconciliation,' 'three-statement,' and the specific ERP (NetSuite, SAP, Oracle, Sage Intacct, QuickBooks). Listing 'experience with accounting' without these phrases sinks the resume.

Mid-level seekers should declare CPA exam parts passed (AUD, FAR, REG, BEC or the new CPA Evolution sections), Excel modeling depth (XLOOKUP, pivot tables, Power Query), and audit-support history. Senior reviewers want close-cycle days, account reconciliation volumes, and SOX-control ownership. Generic 'managed financial statements' bullets without dollar amounts or close-day metrics get cut.

The 12-point ATS checklist for Accountants

  1. Declare CPA status with parts passed and dateWrite 'CPA Candidate: AUD and FAR passed 2024, REG scheduled Q2 2025' or 'CPA licensed, Illinois #123456, active 2021-present'. Recruiters filter by CPA status as a binary, then by parts.
  2. Name ERP system, modules, and company sizeSpecify 'NetSuite OneWorld (multi-subsidiary), SAP S/4HANA FI/CO, Oracle Fusion, Sage Intacct, QuickBooks Online Advanced' with the company revenue band you supported. ERP plus scale is a tight ATS filter.
  3. Quantify month-end close days and improvementsQuote 'closed books in 5 business days, reduced from 9, across 14 entities' rather than 'managed month-end close'. Close-day metrics rank candidates instantly.
  4. List specific reconciliations ownedSpecify 'reconciled 38 GL accounts monthly including intercompany, prepaids, accrued liabilities, fixed assets per ASC 360' to prove ownership beyond cash and credit cards.
  5. Cite US GAAP and FASB topic numbersReference 'revenue recognition per ASC 606, lease accounting per ASC 842, stock comp per ASC 718' instead of 'GAAP compliance'. Topic numbers signal real technical accounting fluency.
  6. Show Excel depth beyond pivot tablesList 'XLOOKUP, INDEX/MATCH, SUMIFS, Power Query, Power Pivot, dynamic arrays, three-statement linkage in Excel'. Modern accounting analysts are filtered for Power Query specifically.
  7. Include audit-support experienceMention 'prepared PBC schedules for Big 4 audit, walked auditors through revenue cutoff and accrual testing' so external-audit interaction is visible. Senior roles assume this.
  8. List AP/AR volumes and aging metricsQuote 'processed 1,200 AP invoices monthly via Bill.com, kept DSO under 38 days on $42M annual AR' so transaction-cycle scale is concrete.
  9. Show journal entry volume and SOX control workSpecify 'prepared and reviewed 180 journal entries monthly per SOX control AC-04, segregation-of-duties matrix maintained in Workday'. SOX control IDs prove process maturity.
  10. Include sales tax, payroll, or treasury exposureNote 'managed multi-state sales tax via Avalara across 27 nexus states, supported ADP payroll for 240 EEs, bank reconciliations in Treasury Workstation' to expand the cycle coverage you can step into.
  11. List BI or close-management toolsMention 'BlackLine for reconciliations and task management, FloQast close checklist, Vena for budgeting, Tableau for monthly variance dashboards'. Modern controllers want close-management tooling experience.
  12. Quantify cost savings and process improvementsQuote 'automated 14 recurring journal entries via NetSuite SuiteScript, cut close prep time by 11 hours/month' so the value goes beyond keeping the books.

Common ATS rejection reasons for Accountants

✗ CPA status unclear or buried
Fix:Add a dedicated line: 'CPA Candidate, 3 of 4 sections passed (AUD, FAR, BEC), REG scheduled June 2025' so recruiters can score the path.
✗ ERP listed generically as 'accounting software'
Fix:Replace with 'NetSuite OneWorld, multi-subsidiary consolidation, custom saved searches and SuiteAnalytics' to prove platform depth.
✗ No close-day or reconciliation volume metrics
Fix:Quote 'closed in 4 business days, owned 28 BS reconciliations, zero post-close adjustments in 2024' to make close performance rankable.
✗ GAAP referenced without ASC topic numbers
Fix:Add 'revenue per ASC 606 (5-step model), leases per ASC 842 ROU asset, deferred rent migration' to prove technical accounting work.
✗ Excel claimed at expert level with no functions named
Fix:List 'Power Query for AR aging, Power Pivot data models, XLOOKUP and INDEX/MATCH, three-statement model' so interviewers can test exact depth.
✗ SOX or internal controls mentioned without control IDs or owner role
Fix:Specify 'owned 6 SOX controls across O2C cycle, walkthroughs with PwC, zero deficiencies in 2024 audit' so SOX experience is concrete.

Three example resume bullets for a Accountant

Patterns a strong Accountant bullet should hit: action verb at the start, role-specific noun in the middle, measurable number at the end. Adapt these to your real work; do not copy verbatim.

FAQ — Accountant ATS questions

Do I list 'CPA Candidate' or wait until I pass all parts?

List it the moment you sit for your first section. Format as 'CPA Candidate: FAR passed Aug 2024 (score 82), AUD scheduled Nov 2024.' Recruiters filter for the CPA keyword broadly and reward visible progress. Hiding partial completion looks like you have not started.

Should I list QuickBooks if I am applying to a NetSuite or SAP shop?

Yes, but de-emphasize it. Lead with the enterprise ERP (NetSuite, SAP, Oracle) and put QuickBooks in a secondary 'Also experienced with' line. Hiring managers at mid-market and enterprise companies sometimes assume QuickBooks-only candidates cannot scale into multi-subsidiary consolidation.

How do I show SOX experience if I worked at a private company?

Frame it as 'SOX-equivalent internal controls' and describe the segregation-of-duties matrix, approval thresholds, and ITGCs you maintained. Mention if the company was prepping for IPO or a buy-side diligence; that signals public-company readiness without overclaiming.

Is it worth listing my Big 4 internship from 8 years ago?

Keep it on a one-line basis in the experience section. Big 4 brand still carries weight in screening, and audit-side exposure (PBC, walkthroughs, sampling) is signal that you understand how external auditors work, which makes you faster to onboard at any controller's office.

Should I separate AP/AR experience from full-cycle accounting?

Yes if you are targeting senior accountant or controller roles. Show clearly which years were transactional (AP clerk, AR specialist) versus full-cycle (close ownership, financial statements, audit support). Recruiters discount candidates whose only experience is transactional even if the year count looks senior.
