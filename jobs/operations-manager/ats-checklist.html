Operations Manager ATS Resume Checklist (2026)

12 ATS-resume checks Operations Managers need to pass in 2026, the keywords recruiters scan for, and three role-specific resume bullets to copy.

Operations Manager resumes get screened against P&L responsibility, headcount, and cost-out numbers before a human reads a single line. ATS rules used by mid-market and enterprise employers look for explicit dollar amounts under management, span-of-control headcount, and process methodology tags like Lean, Six Sigma, or Kaizen. Generic 'oversaw operations' phrasing routes you to the no-pile.

Hiring managers want to see throughput, cost-per-unit, on-time delivery, and KPI dashboard ownership written as specific deltas (before vs after). Show the ERP/MRP system you ran the floor on (NetSuite, SAP, Oracle), the SOPs you wrote or rewrote, and the vendor contracts you negotiated. Capacity planning, S&OP cadence, and headcount forecasting are the differentiators between a coordinator-level resume and a true Ops Manager profile.

The 12-point ATS checklist for Operations Managers

  1. State P&L size in dollarsLead with the budget or revenue P&L you owned (e.g., '$18M annual opex'). ATS scoring weights numeric scope; vague 'managed budget' lines underperform against quantified peers in stack-rank screens.
  2. Quantify headcount and span of controlList direct reports, total team, and number of supervisors/leads under you. Recruiters filter for 'managed 25+ FTE' or '3 shifts' to confirm Manager-level scope versus Supervisor-level scope.
  3. Name your ERP/MRP system explicitlySpell out SAP S/4HANA, Oracle NetSuite, Microsoft Dynamics, or Infor instead of 'ERP system'. Boolean searches by hiring managers and agencies key on the exact platform name.
  4. Show Lean Six Sigma certification levelYellow, Green, Black Belt, or Master Black Belt — list the belt color and certifying body (ASQ, IASSC). Ops Manager job descriptions filter on belt level for continuous-improvement roles.
  5. Document a specific cost-out projectName one Kaizen, value-stream-mapping, or 5S project with the dollar savings or hours-saved result. 'Reduced unit cost 12% saving $340K annually' beats 'drove cost savings' every time.
  6. Include on-time delivery or OTIF percentageManufacturing and distribution Ops roles screen for OTIF, fill rate, or schedule attainment. State your baseline and your improved number with the time window (e.g., 'lifted OTIF from 86% to 97% in 9 months').
  7. List SOP authorship and rolloutCount of SOPs written, departments rolled out to, and training hours delivered. SOP ownership signals you can institutionalize process, not just react to fires.
  8. Quantify vendor and contract managementNumber of vendors managed, total spend, and any RFP or contract renegotiation savings. Include 3PL, MRO, or co-packer relationships if applicable.
  9. Show KPI dashboard ownershipName the BI tool (Power BI, Tableau, Looker) and the KPIs you reported weekly to leadership. Ops Managers who built or owned the dashboard rank above those who merely consumed reports.
  10. Capture safety incident rateTRIR, DART, or recordables per 200K hours if you operate in warehouse, manufacturing, or field ops. Safety is a top-five screening criterion for plant Ops Manager reqs.
  11. Reference S&OP or capacity planning cadenceMention monthly S&OP, demand-planning meetings, or capacity models you built in Excel/Anaplan. Senior Ops roles expect cross-functional planning experience, not just execution.
  12. Use action verbs Standardized, Scaled, ConsolidatedThese verbs map to the operational maturity ATS resume parsers tag as 'leadership-level'. Avoid 'helped', 'assisted', or 'participated' which down-rank you into individual-contributor pools.

Common ATS rejection reasons for Operations Managers

✗ No dollar figures attached to budget or savings
Fix:Add P&L size, opex managed, and at least two cost-out projects with dollar results. Numbers convert generic 'managed operations' lines into screenable scope.
✗ Missing ERP/WMS system names
Fix:Spell out SAP, Oracle, NetSuite, Microsoft Dynamics, or the specific WMS. Recruiters Boolean-search the platform string verbatim.
✗ Headcount and span of control omitted
Fix:State direct reports, total team, and number of supervisors. Without headcount you read as a coordinator, not a manager.
✗ Lean/Six Sigma credentials buried or unlisted
Fix:Move belt certification to the top of a Certifications block. CI-heavy employers filter on 'Green Belt' or 'Black Belt' as a knockout keyword.
✗ Process improvement claims with no methodology
Fix:Tag each improvement with the framework used — Kaizen, DMAIC, value-stream mapping, 5S — so the bullet maps to a vocabulary the hiring manager already uses.

FAQ — Operations Manager ATS questions

Should I list P&L size if I only had budget responsibility, not full P&L?

Yes — label it precisely as 'opex budget owned' or 'cost-center budget' rather than 'P&L'. Recruiters respect accurate scope and penalize inflated titles. The dollar number still passes the ATS scope filter even without revenue responsibility.

Do I need Six Sigma certification to apply for Ops Manager roles?

Not always, but Green Belt is the most common floor for mid-market manufacturing and distribution. For tech, healthcare, or service ops, methodology exposure (Agile, Kanban, OKRs) often substitutes. List any formal training even if you didn't certify.

How should I write headcount when I had matrix or dotted-line reports?

Separate direct reports from dotted-line. Example: '12 direct reports, 60 dotted-line across 4 sites.' Hiring managers see through inflated headcount and prize clarity over volume.

Is the WMS or ERP system worth listing if I only used it as an end user?

Yes, but qualify your depth — 'power user, ran cycle counts and built 14 custom reports in NetSuite' beats listing 12 systems with no context. Recruiters ask about depth on screens.

Should continuous-improvement projects go in the bullets or a separate section?

Inline in the bullets for the relevant role. A separate 'Projects' section works only if you have 3+ that materially changed cost, throughput, or quality. Otherwise it splits attention.
