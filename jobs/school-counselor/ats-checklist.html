School Counselor ATS Resume Checklist (2026)

12 ATS-resume checks School Counselors need to pass in 2026, the keywords recruiters scan for, and three role-specific resume bullets to copy.

School Counselor resumes are screened by district HR for state credential (PPS Services in California, School Counselor Endorsement in Texas, etc.), master's degree in counseling or related field, and ASCA National Model fluency. Caseload size is the universal scope metric — counselors with 350+ caseloads at Title I sites get different consideration than those at 180:1 ratios in well-resourced districts.

Principals and district leads look for crisis-intervention experience (suicide assessment, mandated-reporting volume), college and career counseling outcomes (FAFSA completion, college-going rate, A-G completion), SEL program implementation, and group-counseling delivery. Bilingual capacity, restorative practices training, and any experience with Naviance, SCOIR, or MaiaLearning for college planning are differentiators.

The 12-point ATS checklist for School Counselors

  1. State credential and authorization explicitlyPPS (Pupil Personnel Services) credential in CA, School Counselor Endorsement in TX, LSSC in other states. Include level (elementary, secondary, K-12) and state.
  2. List master's degree program and CACREP accreditationM.Ed. in School Counseling, MA in Counseling Psychology. CACREP accreditation is increasingly preferred and worth noting if your program holds it.
  3. Quantify caseload sizeStudents per counselor (e.g., '380:1 caseload at Title I comprehensive high school'). Caseload defines workload realism and is the #1 scope filter.
  4. Show ASCA National Model implementationASCA Model alignment, RAMP designation (Recognized ASCA Model Program) if your school holds it, and any direct-vs-indirect service ratio you maintained.
  5. Document crisis intervention experienceSuicide risk assessments conducted (Columbia Protocol, ASIST trained), mandated reports filed, threat assessments. Crisis bench strength is essential for secondary roles.
  6. Quantify college and career counseling outcomesFAFSA completion rate, college-going rate, A-G or graduation requirements completion, AP/IB enrollment growth. Outcomes carry the resume.
  7. Detail group and individual counseling deliveryNumber of groups run, topics (grief, anxiety, anger management, college prep), individual session volume. Direct service is what counselors actually do daily.
  8. List SEL program implementationSecond Step, Sanford Harmony, MindUP, Choose Love, Character Strong. Naming a curriculum signals trained delivery, not improvisation.
  9. Show 504 plan and IEP collaboration504 coordinator role if held, IEP team participation, mental-health goal authorship. Counselors who collaborate with SPED rank above pure-academic counselors.
  10. Reference college-planning platformsNaviance, SCOIR, MaiaLearning, Common App, CSS Profile, Cal-SOAP. Platform fluency is essential for HS counselor roles.
  11. Mention multi-tiered system of supports (MTSS)Tier 1, 2, and 3 support delivery and any data-team or MTSS leadership role. Districts increasingly screen for MTSS framework fluency.
  12. Avoid 'love working with students' fillerCounseling resumes win on credential, caseload, methodology, and outcomes — not unmeasurable enthusiasm.

Common ATS rejection reasons for School Counselors

✗ Credential not named (PPS, endorsement)
Fix:Add specific state credential abbreviation and authorization level in header. Credential mismatch is the first knockout in school-counselor hiring.
✗ Caseload size missing
Fix:Quantify students per counselor for every role. Caseload defines work realism — without it, principals assume the worst.
✗ No crisis-intervention training listed
Fix:Add Columbia Protocol, ASIST, QPR, or district-specific suicide-assessment training. Secondary counselor roles require crisis bench.
✗ College outcomes not quantified
Fix:Add FAFSA completion, college-going rate, or A-G/IB completion percentages. Outcomes are the strongest evidence of effectiveness.
✗ No SEL curriculum or framework named
Fix:Name Second Step, MindUP, Choose Love, or whatever you've delivered. Curriculum naming signals trained practice.

FAQ — School Counselor ATS questions

How do I show crisis-intervention work without breaking confidentiality?

Aggregate by type and volume — 'conducted 30+ suicide risk assessments using Columbia Protocol with appropriate parental notification and external referrals' — never name students, situations, or identifiable details. Districts respect that boundary and read aggregate competence positively.

Is RAMP designation worth pursuing or noting?

Yes, especially if applying to ASCA-aligned districts or states (California, North Carolina, others). RAMP signals you've implemented a measurable ASCA Model program, not just attended training. Lead-counselor and director roles often list RAMP experience as preferred.

Should I list every counseling theory or modality?

List those you genuinely use in school settings — CBT-informed, solution-focused brief therapy, motivational interviewing, trauma-informed. Avoid listing modalities you only studied; principals interview on real delivery.

How do I show impact at the elementary level where college outcomes don't apply?

Use SEL program outcomes (referral reductions, attendance gains), behavioral data (office referrals, suspensions), and 504/IEP collaboration metrics. Elementary impact is measured through climate and behavior data, not college metrics.

Do I need bilingual capacity for school counselor roles?

Highly preferred in districts with significant Spanish-speaking populations and often listed as 'desired' or required for specific sites. Even basic Spanish proficiency for parent communication is worth listing honestly with proficiency level.
