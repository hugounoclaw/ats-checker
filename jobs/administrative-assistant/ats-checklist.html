Administrative Assistant ATS Resume Checklist (2026)

12 ATS-resume checks Administrative Assistants need to pass in 2026, the keywords recruiters scan for, and three role-specific resume bullets to copy.

Administrative Assistant resumes get filtered for specific tool fluency, executive-support tenure, and confidentiality signals before a hiring manager glances at them. MS Office Suite (specifically advanced Outlook, Excel, PowerPoint), Google Workspace, Concur or SAP Concur for expenses, and calendar-management at executive scale are the foundational keyword set. ATS scans key on years supporting C-suite or VP-level executives because that scope determines pay band.

Hiring managers want concrete numbers: how many executives supported, calendar density (meetings per week), travel arrangements per year, and any board-meeting or investor-meeting prep work. Discretion and confidentiality are assumed but worth reinforcing through context (legal, finance, healthcare, executive coaching). Strong admin profiles also show project-management software (Asana, Monday, Trello), DocuSign, and any expense-card reconciliation volume.

The 12-point ATS checklist for Administrative Assistants

  1. State the executive level supportedC-suite, VP, Director — and how many simultaneously (e.g., 'EA to CFO and 2 SVPs'). Scope of support is the single biggest factor in EA/Admin pay bands.
  2. Quantify calendar and inbox volumeMeetings per week scheduled, inbox volume managed, time-zone complexity. 'Managed 65+ meetings/week across 9 time zones' converts soft 'calendar management' into screenable scope.
  3. Name MS Office Suite version and depthMicrosoft 365, Outlook (advanced rules and delegation), Excel (pivot tables, vlookups), PowerPoint (executive-ready decks). 'Proficient in Office' is too vague — depth signals matter.
  4. List Concur or expense-reporting platformSAP Concur, Expensify, Ramp, Brex — and monthly expense volume processed. Expense reconciliation is one of the highest-frequency EA tasks.
  5. Document travel-coordination volumeTrips booked per year, international vs domestic, and any visa or executive-protection coordination. International travel adds a 10-15% pay premium for EA roles.
  6. Show project-management tool experienceAsana, Monday.com, Trello, ClickUp, Notion. Modern admin roles increasingly involve project tracking beyond pure scheduling.
  7. Highlight discretion and confidentiality contextLegal, finance, healthcare, executive coaching, or board-level work where confidentiality is contractually required. NDAs and discretion proof points reassure hiring managers.
  8. Detail board or investor meeting prepQuarterly board prep, investor decks coordinated, materials assembled, minutes taken. Board-level admin work is a tier above standard EA scope.
  9. Cover gatekeeper and routing volumeDaily call/email volume triaged, vendor-request routing, internal-FAQ ownership. Gatekeeping is the invisible 40% of the job — worth surfacing.
  10. List meeting tech stack (Zoom, Teams, Webex)Plus webinar platforms (Zoom Webinar, ON24) if you've run large internal events. Hybrid-work admin roles screen for video-platform admin chops.
  11. Include tenure with one executiveLong tenure with a single principal signals trust and is a known signal in executive-search. 'Supported CFO for 6 years through 2 acquisitions' is gold.
  12. Avoid 'self-starter, team player' fillerEA roles are won on tool depth, scope, and tenure — not generic soft skills. Every line should carry an executive level, volume, or platform.

Common ATS rejection reasons for Administrative Assistants

✗ No executive level or scope stated
Fix:Lead each role with the level supported (CFO, SVP) and number of executives. Scope is the primary driver of EA pay tier and the first filter recruiters apply.
✗ Generic 'Microsoft Office' without depth
Fix:Specify advanced Outlook features (delegation, rules), Excel skills (pivot tables, VLOOKUP), and PowerPoint executive deck experience. Depth differentiates.
✗ Expense platform not named
Fix:Add Concur, Expensify, Ramp, or Brex with monthly volume. Expense work is a high-frequency task hiring managers screen for explicitly.
✗ Travel coordination described without volume or geography
Fix:Quantify trips per year and international scope. International travel is a major EA differentiator.
✗ No board, investor, or confidential-work context
Fix:If you've supported board, investor, M&A, or legal-privileged work, name it (within confidentiality limits). This work commands higher offers.

FAQ — Administrative Assistant ATS questions

Should I list every executive I've supported individually?

List by title and tenure rather than name (unless the principal is a publicly recognized figure and you have permission). 'Supported the CFO for 4 years, then transitioned to CEO support during succession' shows trajectory better than a bulleted name list.

How do I show discretion without breaking confidentiality?

Describe the context (privileged legal matters, M&A diligence, executive succession) without disclosing specifics. 'Handled NDA-protected board materials through 2 acquisitions' is sufficient signal without breaching anything.

Do I need a specific certification like CAP or PACE?

Not required, but the CAP (Certified Administrative Professional) and Microsoft Office Specialist certs add a tangible credential, especially for admins moving from coordinator to EA roles. They appear in many JD 'preferred' sections.

Is it worth listing typing speed or office equipment?

Typing speed is dated; skip unless you support court reporting or transcription. Office equipment knowledge is assumed. Use that resume space for software depth and executive scope instead.

How do I handle long tenure with one executive who has now left?

Long tenure is a strength — surface it. 'EA to CFO for 6 years through 2 funding rounds and one acquisition; transitioning as CFO retires Q2 2026' gives context, shows continuity, and explains the search.
