What ATS systems actually scan for in a Paralegal resume

Legal employers run most applications through Workday, Greenhouse, or iCIMS before a human ever opens them, and legal recruiters configure those systems to match on tool names and litigation-stage vocabulary — not generic phrases like "supported attorneys." The parser looks for the exact research platform (Westlaw vs. LexisNexis), the e-filing system (PACER or a state portal), and where you sit in the case lifecycle — discovery, deposition prep, trial preparation.

The biggest tailoring decision is litigation vs. transactional. A litigation posting front-loads discovery, subpoenas, document review, and trial prep; a corporate or transactional role wants contract management, due diligence, and closing binders. Pull the posting into the JD keyword extractor and mirror the practice area (family, IP, immigration, PI) and platform it names — those are usually the highest-weighted terms.

Legal hiring is credential-sensitive, so ATS rules frequently gate on certifications. If the posting asks for a Certified Paralegal or Notary, put the exact credential on its own line. For the mechanics behind this scoring, see how ATS software actually works.

Why Paralegal resumes get auto-rejected

  • Writing "legal software" instead of naming Westlaw, LexisNexis, Relativity, or PACER — the ATS matches exact product names from the JD.
  • Omitting the practice area or litigation stage, which tanks keyword density for that specific posting.
  • Dumping skills in a trailing list the parser de-weights versus the same terms used inside experience bullets.
  • Two-column or table "designed" layouts that scramble the parser's reading order.

FAQ: ATS keywords for Paralegals

Do I need both Westlaw and LexisNexis on my resume?
Only the platforms you have actually used — but match whichever one the job description names, since many firms standardize on a single research tool.

Are litigation and corporate paralegal keywords the same?
No. Litigation postings emphasize discovery, depositions, and trial prep; transactional roles emphasize contracts, due diligence, and closings. Run each JD through the extractor separately.

Will listing a certification help with the ATS?
Yes, when the posting requests it. Add the exact credential (for example, "Certified Paralegal (NALA)") on a Certifications line so the parser captures it.
