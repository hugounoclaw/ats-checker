What ATS systems actually scan for in a Warehouse Supervisor resume

Warehouse and distribution roles get screened through high-volume systems like Workday and iCIMS, and the filters lean hard on three things: the specific WMS platform you've run (Manhattan, SAP EWM, Oracle WMS, HighJump), the safety credentials the role requires (OSHA, forklift certified), and the continuous-improvement methodology the site uses (Lean, 5S, Kaizen). Naming the actual WMS matters — "warehouse management system" as a generic phrase will miss a filter set to the exact product.

Because this is a supervisory role, the ATS and the recruiter are also looking for leadership signal that an associate resume won't have: team size, scheduling, cross-training, and performance metrics. If your bullets read like a picker rather than a leader, you'll clear the keyword filter but stall at the human screen.   • Led Lean, 5S, and Kaizen initiatives that cut pick-travel time 18% and lifted performance metrics across the floor.
  • Maintained OSHA safety compliance with zero lost-time incidents over 14 months as a forklift certified trainer.
  • Ran WMS transactions in SAP via RF scanners while coordinating freight and vendor schedules.

Why Warehouse Supervisor resumes get auto-rejected

  • Saying "warehouse management system" when the JD names a specific WMS (Manhattan, SAP EWM, Oracle).
  • Missing the safety gate — OSHA and forklift certification are often hard-required filters.
  • Reading like an associate: no team size, scheduling, or cross-training keywords to signal supervision.
  • No metrics, when ops recruiters filter for throughput, accuracy, and safety numbers.

FAQ: ATS keywords for Warehouse Supervisors

Which WMS should I name on my resume?
The one you've actually used — but mirror the system the JD names (Manhattan, SAP EWM, Oracle WMS, HighJump). To the keyword filter they're not interchangeable.

Is "forklift certified" really an ATS keyword?
Yes. Forklift certification and OSHA are frequently set as required filters for warehouse roles, so write them exactly as the posting does.

How is a supervisor resume different from an associate one?
Supervisor versions need team-size, scheduling, cross-training, and performance-metrics language to clear leadership filters. Run the JD through the extractor to confirm the emphasis.
